شين شيه ص

1. ⇒ شيه ⇒ شاه

شَاهَهُ, aor. يَشِيهُهُ, (Ḳ,) inf. n. شَيْهٌ, (TA,) i. q. عَانَهُ, (Ibn-Buzurj, Ḳ, TA, [in the CK, erroneously عابَهُ,]) i. e. He smote him with the [evil] eye. (TA.) [See also 1 in art. شوه.]

شَيْهٌ / شِيهٌ

شَيْهٌ and شِيهٌ: see شَاةٌ (of which they are quasi-pl. ns.) in art. شوه.

شِيَاهٌ

شِيَاهٌ: see شَاةٌ (of which it is a pl.) in art. شوه.

شَيُوهٌ

شَيُوهٌ That smites vehemently with the [evil] eye. (Ibn-Buzurj, Ḳ, TA. [In the CK, عَيُوبٌ is erroneously put for عَيُونٌ.])

شَيِّهٌ

شَيِّهٌ: see شَاةٌ (of which it is a quasi-pl. n.) in art. شوه.

أَشْيَهُ

أَشْيَهُ [More, and most, wont to smite with the evil eye]. One says, هُوَ مِنْ أَشْيَهِ النَّاسِ [He is of the most wont, of men, to smite with the evil eye: this meaning being indicated by the context]. (Ibn-Buzurj, Ḳ, TA.)
