1. ⇒ شرط

شَرَطَ عَلَيْهِ كَذَا, (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ,) aor. ـِ {يَشْرِطُ} and ـُ {يَشْرُطُ}, (Ṣ, Mṣb,) inf. n. شَرْطٌ; (Mṣb;) andاشترط↓ عليه كذا; (Ṣ,* Mṣb,* Ḳ,* TA;) both signify the same; (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ;) [He imposed such a thing as a condition, or by stipulation, upon him;] he made such a thing a condition against him. And it is said in a prov., الشَّرْطُ أَمْلَكُ عَلَيْكَ أَمْ لَكَ (TA) The condition is most valid, or binding, [whether it be against thee or in thy favour:] (Mgh in art. ملك:) relating to the keeping of conditions between brothers.
