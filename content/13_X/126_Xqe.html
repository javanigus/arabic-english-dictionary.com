شقو شقى شك

1. ⇒ شقى

شَقَى, inf. n. شَقْىٌ, said of the نَاب [or tush] of a camel, It grew forth: a dial. var. of شَقَأَ. (Ḳ in art. شقو [but belonging to the present art.: mentioned in the TA, as not in the Ḳ, on the authority of ISd].)

Root: شقى - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

[Freytag states, as on the authority of Abu-l-ʼAlà, that شَقَى is used for شَقَّ He clave, or split,, &c.:]

Root: شقى - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

[and that Reiske has explained it as signifying He opened the mouth with a wide grinning (“amplo rictu”).]

شَاقٍ

شَاقٍ A long prominence, or projecting portion, or ledge, (حَيْدٌ طَالِعٌ طَوِيلٌ,) of a mountain, (Ḳ, TA,) that cannot be ascended: (TA:) by rule it should be with ء [i. e. شَاقِئٌ, as being derived from شَقَأَ]: (Ṣgh, TA:) pl. شَوَاقِى (Ḳ [correctly شَوَاقٍ]) and شُقْيَانٌ. (TA. [Mentioned in the Ḳ in art. شقو, but belonging to the present art.])
