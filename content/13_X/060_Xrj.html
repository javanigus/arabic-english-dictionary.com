3. ⇒ شارج

مُشَارَجَةٌ The being like, one to another. (O, Ḳ.) One says, شارجهُ He was like to him; or it, to it: and شَارَجَا They two were like, each to the other. (TḲ.)

(Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: شرج - Entry: شَرْجٌ ＝ Dissociation: B

Also A party, or distinct body or class [of men]. (Ṣ, Ḳ.) One says, أَصْبَحُوا فِى هٰذَا الأَمْرِ شَرْجَيْنِ i. e. [They became, in this affair,] two parties. (Ṣ.) Root: شرج - Entry: شَرْجٌ ― Signification: B3
