1. ⇒ رفّ

رَفَّ, (T,) or رَفَّ لَوْنُهُ, (Ṣ, M, O, Ḳ,) aor. ـِ {يَرْفِفُ}, inf. n. رَفٌّ and رَفِيفٌ, Its colour shone, or glistened; (T, Ṣ, M, O, Ḳ;) said of a thing; (T;) as alsoارتفّ↓. (Ḳ.) Thus رَفَّتْ أَسْنَانُهُ, His teeth shone, or glistened. (M.) El-Aạshà says, describing the front teeth of a woman,

* وَمَهًا تَرِفُّ غُرُوبُهُ *
* يَشْفِى المُتَيَّمَ ذَا الحَرَارَهْ *

[And clean, white, lustrous front teeth, the abundance of their saliva shining, or glistening; that would cure of his malady the enslaved by love who has burning in his heart]. (T, O, Ṣ.) And one says also, رَفَّ البَرْقُ,, aor. ـِ {يَرْفِفُ} and ـُ {يَرْفُفُ}, (M,) inf. n. رَفٌّ, (M, Ḳ,) The lightning gleamed, or shone; or flashed faintly, and then disappeared, and then flashed again. (M, Ḳ.*)

Root: رف - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

(T in art. ورف.)
