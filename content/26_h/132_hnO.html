هن هنأ هنب

1. ⇒ هنأ

هَنُؤَ, aor. ـُ {يَهْنُأُ}, inf. n. هَنَآءَةٌ; and هَنِئَ, aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ}; It came, or happened, without inconvenience, or trouble: (Ḳ:) [it was pleasant, or productive of enjoyment: see what immediately follows].

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

هَنُؤَ الطَّعَامُ (Ṣ, Ḳ *) aor. ـُ {يَهْنُأُ}, inf. n. هَنَآءَةٌ (Ṣ, Ḳ) and هَنَأَةٌ and هَنْءٌ, (Ḳ,) or هِنْءٌ (as in some copies of the Ḳ, and in the L); epithet هَنِىْءٌ; (Ṣ;) and هَنِئَ, (Akh, Ṣ, Ḳ,) aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ}, inf. n. هَنْءٌ; (TA;) and هَنَأَ, aor. ـِ {يَهْنِأُ}, (Lth,) The food was, or became, pleasant, or productive of enjoyment, to the eater: or easy to swallow; not attended by trouble: [agreeable:] or not succeeded by harm, even after digestion. (Z, cited voce مَرُؤَ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

هَنَأَنِى الطَّعَامُ, (Akh, Ṣ, Ḳ), and هَنَأَ لِىَ, aor. ـِ {يَهْنِأُ} and ـَ {يَهْنَأُ} (Ṣ, Ḳ) and ـُ, (Ḳ,) unexampled, says Akh, in the class termed mahmooz, (Ṣ,) [though بَرَأَ and قَرَأَ are similar with respect to their having damm to the aor.,] inf. n. هَنْءٌ and هِنْءٌ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) [The food was pleasant, or productive of enjoyment, to me: or easy to swallow;, &c.: see هَنُؤَ].

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

هَنَأَنِى الطّعامُ وَمَرأَنِى: see art. مرأ.

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A5

هَنَأَهُ ذٰلِكَ, and هنأ لَهُ ذلك That (thing) was pleasant, or productive of enjoyment, to him;, &c. (TA.) [See هَنُؤَ.]

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A6

هَنَأَنِى خَبَرُ فُلَانٍ The news of such a one was pleasant to me to hear. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A7

هَنِئَ الطّعَامَ, aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ}; andتهنّأ↓ بَالطّعامِ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and تهنّأ الطّعام, and استهنأهُ, (TA,) [He enjoyed the food; found it pleasant, or productive of enjoyment;, &c.: see هَنُؤَ:] he found the food to be productive of no evil result, and not attended by inconvenience. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A8

هَنِئَ, (AZ, Ṣ, Ḳ,) aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ}, inf. n. هَنَأٌ and هَنْءٌ, (Ḳ,) He (a beast) lighted upon a good piece of herbage, but did not satiate himself therewith. (AZ, Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A9

أَكَلْنَا هٰذَا الطَّعَامَ حَتَّى هَنِئْنَا مِنْهُ We ate this food until we were satiated with it. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A10

هَنِئَتِ الإِبِلُ The camels were satiated with herbage. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A11

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A12

هَنَأَنَا ٱللّٰهُ الطَّعَامَ [God made the food pleasant, or productive of enjoyment, to us:, &c.: made us to enjoy it: see هَنُؤَ]. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A13

هَنَأَتْنِيهِ العَافِيَةُ [Health made it pleasant, or productive of enjoyment, to me:, &c.]. (Ḳ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A14

لِيَهْنِئْكَ الفَارِسُ [May the horseman give thee joy: a form of congratulation on the exploits of a horseman; i. e., I congratulate thee on the exploits of the horseman]: also written and pronounced لِيَهْنِيكَ: ليهنك, though it occurs in a trad., pronounced لِيَهْنِكَ or لِيَهْنَكَ, (but which pronunciation is to be preferred is disputed,) is said to be a vulgarism, and not allowable. Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A16

هَنَأَهُ, aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ} and ـِ {يَهْنِأُ}, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) inf. n. هَنْءٌ; (Ṣ;) andاهنأهُ↓; (IAạr, Ḳ;) He gave him, or bestowed upon him: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) gave him plentifully. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A17

هَنَأَ الطَّعَامَ, inf. n. هَنْءٌ and هِنْءٌ (Ḳ) and هَنَآءَةٌ (as in some copies of the Ḳ) or هَنَأَةٌ (as in others) or هِنَأَةٌ (as in others) or هَنْأَةٌ (as in the CK), He made the food good; qualified it properly; seasoned it: syn. أَصْلَحَهُ. (Ḳ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A18

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A19

هَنَأَ القَوْمَ, aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ}, He nourished, or maintained, the people; (Ṣ;) satisfied their wants; bestowed upon them. (TA.) Ex. هَنَأَهُمْ شَهْرَيْنِ [He maintained them two months]. Hence the proverb quoted in illustration of the word هَانِئٌ, accord. to the second reading. (TA)

هَنَأَهُ He aided, succoured, or defended, him. (Ḳ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

هَنَأَ الإِبِلَ, aor. ـَ {يَهْنَأُ}, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and ـِ and ـُ, (Ḳ: dev. from constant rule as shown above: TA), inf. n. هَنَأٌ and هَنْءٌ, (TA,) He smeared the camels with هِنَآء, which is tar, or liquid pitch, syn. قَطِرَان, (AZ, Ṣ, Ḳ,) or a kind thereof, (TA,) [as a remedy for, or preservative against, the mange, or scab].

Root: هنأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B2

لَيْسَ الهَنْءُ بِالدَّسِّ The smearing of a camel [all over] with هِنَآء is not [merely] smearing the cavities under the shoulders, and the like, which the mange, or scab, more quickly attacks. A proverb, applied to him who does not a thing thoroughly. (TA.)

2. ⇒ هنّأ

هَنَّاهُ وَمَنَّاهُ (in a trad. respecting the prostration for inattention) He (the devil) made him to think of pleasant things, or things productive of enjoyment, and of things wished for, or objects of desire, in his prayer. The former verb is pronounced thus to assimilate it to the latter. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A2

هنّأهُ بِالأَمْرِ, inf. n. تَهْنِئَةٌ and تَهْنِىْءٌ; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) andهَنَأَهُ↓, (Ḳ,) inf. n. هَنْءٌ; (TA;) He congratulated him on the thing, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) such as the possession of a government, &c.: (Ṣ:) he said to him لِيَهْنِئْكَ [May it give thee joy]. (Ḳ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A3

[When the agent of the verb is God, the meaning necessarily is, He granted him enjoyment in the thing; made him to have enjoyment in it.]

Root: هنأ - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A4

هُنِّئْتَ وَلَا تُنْكَهْ: see art. نكأ.

هَنِىْءٌ

هَنِىْءٌ What comes or happens to one without inconvenience, or trouble: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) [what is pleasant, or productive of enjoyment; an unalloyed gratification, i. e., a thing that gives unalloyed enjoyment; see what follows:] as alsoمَهْنَأٌ↓, (Ḳ,) a subst., sometimes written and pronounced مَهْنَا; pl. مَهَانِئُ, sometimes written and pronounced مَهَانٍ. (TA.) [See مهنأ also below.]

Root: هنأ - Entry: هَنِىْءٌ ― Signification: A2

Pleasant, or productive of enjoyment, to the eater: or easy to swallow; not attended by trouble: or not succeeded by harm, even after its digestion. (Z, cited voce مَرُؤَ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: هَنِىْءٌ ― Signification: A3

هَنِيْئًا مَرِيْئًا [May it be, or Eat it, or Drink it, with enjoyment, and with wholesome result: or with ease in the swallowing, and with quickness in digesting:, &c.: see مَرُؤَ]. (Ṣ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: هَنِىْءٌ ― Signification: A4

هَنِيْئًا لَهُ ذٰلِكَ [May that be productive of enjoyment to him!]. (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: هَنِىْءٌ ― Signification: A5

هَنِيْئًا and مَرِيْئًا are of the number of epithets which are employed after the manner of inf. ns. significant of a prayer or good wish, governed in the acc. case by a verb understood. (Sb.)

هُنَيْئَةٌ

هُنَيْئَةٌ (Ḳ) and هُنَيَّةٌ and هُنَيْهَةٌ (the second is the most usual; and the third is said to be formed by substituting ه for ء; but accord. to some, the word is incorrectly written with ء, [so says F,] and is a dim. formed from هَنْوَةٌ, which becomes first هُنَيْوَةٌ, and then هُنَيَّةٌ: see art. هنو:) (TA:) A little; a little while. (Ḳ.)

هَانِئٌ

هَانِئٌ A servant. (Ḳ.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: هَانِئٌ ― Signification: A2

هَانِئًا occurs in this sense in a trad.; but the reading commonly known is مَاهِنًا. If right, it is an act. part. n. from هنأ “he gave.” (TA.)

Root: هنأ - Entry: هَانِئٌ ― Signification: A3

إِنَّمَا سُمِّيتَ هَانِئًا لِتَهْنِئَ, or لِتَهْنَأَ; the former is the reading of El-Umawee; the latter, of Ks; Thou art only named Háni (Giver, or Nourisher,) that thou mayest give, accord. to both readings; or that thou mayest nourish, or maintain, and supply people's wants; لتعول وتكفى: (TA:) [such is said to be the meaning of لتهنأ here:] and accord. to El-Umawee, لتهنئ signifies لِتُمْرِئَ, (Ṣ,) [which is app. the same as لتعول]. A proverb: said to him who is known for his beneficence, in order that he may continue to do as he has been wont. (TA.)

مَهْنَأٌ

مَهْنَأٌ: see هَنِئٌ.

Root: هنأ - Entry: مَهْنَأٌ ― Signification: A2

لَكَ المَهْنَأُ, (Ṣ,) and المَهْنَا, (TA,) [Unalloyed gratification to thee!]

Root: هنأ - Entry: مَهْنَأٌ ― Signification: A3

لَكَ المَهْنَأُ وَعَلَيْهِ الوِزْرُ [To thee be unalloyed gratification, and on him be the burden, or sin]: said, accord. to a trad., to one who asked whether he should accept an invitation to eat the food of one who received unlawful interest or profit; and also said with respect to eating the food of a tyrannical intendant. (TA.)
