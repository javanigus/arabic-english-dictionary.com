هرث هرج هرجب

1. ⇒ هرج

هَرَجَ aor. ـِ {يَهْرِجُ}, inf. n. هَرْجٌ He did, acted, or occupied himself much in a thing; (Ṣ, L;) expatiated or took a wide range, in it. (L.) This is the original signification. (Ṣ,)

Root: هرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

هَرَجَ فِى الحِدِيثِ, (aor, ـِ inf. n. هَرْجٌ. Mṣb.) He launched into, and expatiated in. or was diffuse in discourse talk or narration: (Ḳ:) this is the signification in most frequent use: (TA:) or he made a confusion, or confounded, therein. (Ḳ, Mṣb.)

Root: هرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

هَرَج. aor ـِ, and ـُ, inf. n. هَرْجٌ, Multum inivit (Ṣ. L.) or [simply] inivit an cillam suam (Ḳ.)

Root: هرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

هَرَجَ, aor. ـِ {يَهْرِجُ}, (inf. n. هَرْجٌ, TA,) He (a horse) ran much (Ṣ. L:) or ran quickly or swiftly: (Mṣb:) or [simply] ran (Ḳ,)

Root: هرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A5

هَرَجَ النَّاسُ aor. ـِ {يَهْرِجُ}. (inf. n. هَرْجٌ, Ṣ.) The people fell into a state of trial, or civil war or conflict and faction or discord, or discussion, (فِتْنَة,) and confusion, or disorder, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and slaughter. (Ḳ.)

Root: هرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A6

هَرِجَ, aor. ـَ {يَهْرَجُ}, (inf. n. هَرَجٌ, Ṣ,) He (a camel) became perplexed in his sight, by reason of the vehemence of heat, and his being much smeared with pitch, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and being heavily laden. (TA.)

2. ⇒ هرّج

هرّج البَعِيرَ, inf. n. تَهْرِيجٌ, andاهرجهُ↓, inf. n. إِهْرَاجٌ; He incited, or urged, the camel to journey on (during the hottest time of the day, Ṣ) until he [the camel] became perplexed so his sight by reason of the vehemence of the beat. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: هرج - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A2

هرّج بِالسَّبْعِ, inf. n. تَهْرِيجٌ, He cried and to the lion or other beast of prey, and child him. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: هرج - Entry: 2. ＝ Dissociation: B

هرّج. inf. n. تَهْرِيجٌ. It (beverage of the kind called نَبِيذ) affected, or took effect upon, a person. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

4. ⇒ اهرج

see 2.

Root: هرج - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A2

أَهْرَجَ The heal reached has (a camel's) inside (L.)

6. ⇒ تهارج

تهارجوا Iniverunt, ulii alias. (TA.)

7. ⇒ انهرج

انهرج He was, or became, affected by beverage of the kind called نَبِيذ. (Ṣ, CK)

هَرْجٌ

هَرْجٌ Trial, or civil war, or conflict and faction, or discord, or dissension. (فِتَنْةٌ,) and confusion. or disorder: (Ṣ:) vehement and much slaughter (TA:) in a trad. respecting the signs of the last day, conflict, and confusion, or disorder: (TA:) or slaughter; as explained by Moḥammad himself: (Ṣ:) and so, accord to Aboo-Moosa. It signifies in the language of Abyssinia (TA.) Ibn-Keys Fr-Rukeiyát said in the days of the faction of Ibn-Ez-Zubayi.

* لَيْت شِعْرِى أَأَوَّلُ الهَرْجِ هٰذَا *
* أَمٌ زَمَانٌ مِنْ فِتْنَةِ غَيْرِ هَرْجِ *

Would that I knew whether this be the first of the slaughter predicted as a sign of the last day or whether it be a time of trial, or civil war,, &c., other than the slaughter so predicted. (Ṣ.)

هَرَّاجٌ

هَرَّاجٌ: see مهْرَجٌ.

هَرَّاجَةٌ

هَرَّاجَةٌ An assembly, or a company, of men who launch into and expatriate in, or are diffuse in, discourse talk, or narration. (Ḳ.)

مُهْرِجٌ

مُهْرِجٌ A man whose camels are affected with the scab, and have therefore been smeared with pitch, and to whose insides the heat has penetrated. (TA.)

مِهْرَجٌ

مِهْرَجٌ andهَرَّاجٌ↓ A horse that runs much: (Ṣ. Ḳ:) andمِهْرَاجٌ↓ a horse that runs vehemently (TA.)

مِهْراجٌ

مِهْراجٌ: see مِهْرجٌ.
