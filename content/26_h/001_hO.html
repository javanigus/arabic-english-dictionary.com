ه هأ هب

R. Q. 1. ⇒ هأهأ

هَأْهَأَ بِالإِبِلِ, (El-Umawee, Ṣ, Ḳ,) inf. n. هِيهَآءٌ and هَأْهَآءٌ, (Ḳ,) the latter extr., (TA,) [see ظَأْظَأَ,] He called the camels to food, or provender, by the cry هِئْ هِئْ: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) or he chid them, (زَجَرَهَا), by the cry هَأْهَأْ. (Ḳ.) [See also arts. جأ and جيأ.]

Root: هأ - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: A2

هَأْهَأَ, inf. n. هَأْهَآءٌ, He chid a dog. (TA.)

Root: هأ - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: A3

He called a dog. (TA.)

Root: هأ - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: A4

هَأْهَأَ, inf. n. هَأْهَآءٌ, He laughed loud and long: [a word imitative of the sound]. (Ḳ, TA.)

هِىْءٌ

هِىْءٌ, subst. from هَأْهَأَ, [A call to camels to food, or provender]. (Ṣ, Ḳ.) هِىْءٌ and جِىْءٌ are said to be thus written by Az's own hand, with kesr: and are thus written in the Jáme' [of Ḳz]. (L.) [See arts. جأ and جيأ.]

هَأْهَأٌ

هَأْهَأٌ and هَأْهَآءٌ One who laughs loud and long. (Ḳ, TA.)

Root: هأ - Entry: هَأْهَأٌ ― Signification: A2

جَارِيَةٌ هَأْهَأَةٌ A damsel who laughs loud and long. (Lḥ, TA.)
