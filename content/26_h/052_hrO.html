هر هرا هرب

1. ⇒ هرأ

هَرَأَهُ البَرْدُ The cold destroyed him, or, perhaps, debilitated him; i. e., a beast; lit., broke him: syn. كسره. (TA.) [See also هَزَأَ.]

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

هَرِئَ, like عُنِىَ, [i. e., pass. in form, but neut. in signification,] (incorrectly written in the Ṣ هَرِئَ, Ḳ,) It (a camel or sheep, &c., or a man.) perished of cold, or heat. (Ks, Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

هَرَأَهُ البَرْدُ, inf. n. هَرْءٌ (Aṣ, Ṣ, Ḳ,) and هَرَآءَةٌ; (Ḳ;) andاهرأهُ↓; (Fr, Ṣ, Ḳ;) The cold affected him so severely as nearly to kill him: (Aṣ, Ṣ, Ḳ:) or so severely as to kill him. (Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

هَرَأَتِ الرِّيحُ The wind was, or became, intensely cold. (Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

هَرَأَ, (Ḳ,) inf. n. هَرْءٌ; (TA:) andهرّأ↓; (Ḳ;) andاهرأ↓, (Fr, Ḳ,) inf. n. إِهْرَآءٌ; (TA;) He cooked flesh-meat thoroughly: (Ḳ:) or so that it fell off from the bone. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B2

هَرِئَ, aor. ـَ {يَهْرَأُ}, inf. n. هَرْءٌ and هُرْءٌ and هُرُوْءٌ; (Ḳ;) andتهرّأ↓; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) It (flesh-meat) was thoroughly cooked: (Ḳ:) or was cooked so as to fall off from the bone. (Ṣ,* TA.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

هَرَأَ فِى مَنْطِقِهِ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) aor. ـَ {يَهْرَأُ}, (Ḳ,) inf. n. هَرْءٌ, (TA,) He was loquacious: or he was loquacious, with incorrectness; (TA;) as also هَرَأَ الكَلَامَ: (ISk, Ṣ:) or he was very foul in his speech: or was very incorrect, or faulty, therein: (Ḳ:) or, simply, he was foul, (Ṣ,) or incorrect, or faulty, (TA,) in his speech: (Ṣ, TA:) alsoاهرأ↓ الكَلَامَ he multiplied his words but spake not to the point, or correctly. (Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: C2

إِنَّ مَنْطِقَهَ يَهْرَأُ, inf. n. هَرْءٌ, [Verily his speech is prolix: or is vitious, and disorderly]. (TA.)

4. ⇒ اهرأ

see 1.

Root: هرأ - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A2

اهرأ فِى الرَّوَاحِ He entered upon the cool time of the evening: (Ṣ, Ḳ:*) or properly said only of the evening of the hot season. (Ḳ.) [See also اهزأ.]

Root: هرأ - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A3

أَهْرِئُ عَنْكَ مِنَ الظَّهِيرَةِ Stay thou until the mid-day heat shall have become assuaged, and the air be cool. (TA, in this art.; and IAạr, in TA, art. فيح.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 4. ＝ Dissociation: B

اهرأ He slew a person. (Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 4. ＝ Dissociation: C

See 1, last sentence but one.

5. ⇒ تهرّأ

تهرّأ He (a beast) was destroyed, or, perhaps, debilitated, by the cold; lit., was broken: syn. تكسّر. (TA.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: 5. ＝ Dissociation: B

See 1.

هَرِئٌ

هَرِئٌ (so in the TA: in one copy of the Ṣ, هَرِىٌ: in another, هَرِىٌّ:) andمُهَرَّأٌ↓ (TA) Meat thoroughly cooked: (TA:) or cooked so as to fall off from the bone. (Ṣ,* TA.)

هُرَأٌ

هُرَأٌ: see هُرَآءٌ.

هُرَآءٌ / هُرَآءَةٌ

هُرَآءٌ, or مَنْطِقٌ هُرَآءٌ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) Loquacity: or vitious, disorderly, speech: (Ḳ:) or loquacity with incorrectness. (Ṣ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: هُرَآءٌ ― Signification: A2

هُرَآءٌ, (Ḳ,) fem. with ة {هُرَآءَةٌ}, pl. with ون; (TA;) andهُرَأٌ↓; (Ḳ;) A great talker of nonsense. (Ḳ.)

هِرَآءٌ

هِرَآءٌ A young shoot of a palm-tree, (AḤn, Ḳ,) when first plucked from the mother-tree. (Aṣ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: هِرَآءٌ ＝ Dissociation: B

الهِرَآءُ A certain devil whose office it is to suggest foul dreams. (Ḳ.)

هَرِيْئَةٌ

قِرَّةٌ لَهَا هَرِيْئَةٌ Cold that occasions injury and death to men and to cattle, &c. (El-Fezáree, ISk, Ṣ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: هَرِيْئَةٌ ― Signification: A2

Also, هَرِيْئَةٌ The time in which cold thus affects them. (TA.)

مَهْرُوْءٌ

مَهْرُوْءٌ, from هُرِئَ, pl. with ون, [in the CK, for مَهْرُوؤُونَ is put مَهْرَؤُنَ,] Perishing of cold, or heat. (Ks, Ḳ.)

Root: هرأ - Entry: مَهْرُوْءٌ ― Signification: A2

Explained by AḤn [in his book on plants] by the words أَلَّذِى قَدْ أَنْضَجَهُ البَرْدُ [meaning, What is nipped, shrunk, shrivelled, or blasted, by the cold: see art. نضج]. (TA.)

مُهَرَّأٌ

مُهَرَّأٌ: see هَرِئٌ.
