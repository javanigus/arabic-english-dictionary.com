هتأ هتر هتك

1. ⇒ هتر

هَتَرَهُ, aor. ـِ {يَهْتِرُ}, (Ḳ, TA,) like يَضْرِبُ, (TA [in the CK, ـ, but this is evidently a mistake,]) It (old age, Ḳ, TA, and disease and grief, TA), made him to be such as is called مُهْتَرٌ; [i. e., made him to lose his reason, or intellect: or to be addicted to, or fond of, speaking of a thing: which latter signification seems to be particularly indicated in the lexicon from which this is taken; but the former seems the more appropriate.] (Ḳ.)

3. ⇒ هاتر

هاترهُ, [inf. n. مُهَاتَرَةٌ and هِتَارٌ,] He encountered him with mutual reviling, saying what was false: (Ḳ,* TA:) so says IAmb, on the authority of AZ; but, says Th, accord. to others, المُهَاتَرَةُ signifies the saying [that] whereof one part contradicts, or annuls, another: and hence one says, دَعِ الهِتَارَ [leave the saying that whereof one part contradicts another]. (TA.)

4. ⇒ اهتر

أُهْتِرَ He became disordered in his intellect: (A, TA:) or he became so by reason of old age: (Ṣ:) or he lost his reason from old age, (AZ, AʼObeyd, Ḳ,) as alsoإِسْتَهْتَرَ↓, (AZ, TA,) or from disease, or grief; as also أَهْتَرَ. (Ḳ.) See also 10, in two places.

5. ⇒ تهتّر

تهتّر He was, or became, stupid, and ignorant. (Ḳ: but only the inf. n. is there mentioned.)

6. ⇒ تهاتر

تهاترا They accused each other falsely. (Ṣ, A, Mgh, Mṣb, Ḳ.)

Root: هتر - Entry: 6. ― Signification: A2

And hence, تهاترت البَيِّنَاتُ, (Mṣb,) and الشَّهَادَاتُ, (A, Mgh,) The testimonies, or evidences, became null: (Mgh, Mṣb:) or belied one another. (A.)

10. ⇒ استهتر

إِسْتَهْتَرَ: see 4.

Root: هتر - Entry: 10. ― Signification: A2

He was, or became, much given to false, or vain, sayings, or actions. (TA [but this seems rather to be أُسْتُهْتِرَ: see its part. n., below.])

Root: هتر - Entry: 10. ― Signification: A3

† He followed his own natural desire, not caring what he did. (Mṣb [but this also seems to be in the pass. form.])

Root: هتر - Entry: 10. ― Signification: A4

أُسْتُهْتِرَ بِكَذَا ‡ He became addicted to, or fond of, such a thing, (Ḳ, TA,) not talking of any other thing, (TA,) nor caring what was done to him, (Ḳ, TA,) nor how he was reviled: (Ḳ:) he became addicted to, or fond of, such a thing, not talking of, nor doing, any other thing: and he became fascinated by such a thing, and lost his reason on account of it, and his strong determination became turned towards it, so that he talked much and vainly respecting it. (TA.)

Root: هتر - Entry: 10. ― Signification: A5

إِسْتَهْتَرَ بِفُلَانَةَ, [or أُسْتُهْتِرَ,] andأَهْتَرَ↓ بِهَا, [or أُهْتِرَ,] ‡ He [became attached, or devoted, to such a woman so that he] cared not what was said of him on her account, nor how he was reviled: (A, TA:) andأُهْتِرَ↓ also signifies † he became addicted or given to, or fond of, speaking of a thing. (Ḳ.)

هُتْرٌ

هُتْرٌ The loss of reason from old age or disease or grief. (Ḳ.)

هِتْرٌ

هِتْرٌ An error in speech. (Ṣ, A, Mgh, Mṣb, Ḳ.) You say, جَآءَ بِهِتْرٍ مِنَ القَوْلِ He uttered an error of speech. (A.) Andهِتْرٌ هَاتِرٌ↓ A great error of speech. (Ṣ, Ḳ.*)

Root: هتر - Entry: هِتْرٌ ― Signification: A2

A falsehood; a lie. (Ḳ.) You say, قَوْلٌ هِتْرٌ A false saying (TA.)

هَاتِرٌ

هَاتِرٌ: see هِتْرٌ.

تَهَاتِرُ

تَهَاتِرُ Testimonies, or evidences, that belie one another: as though pl. of تَهْتَرٌ: (Ḳ:) or any evidences, or testimonies, that are not legal proofs (Mgh [but in my copy of that work written تَهاتُر.])

مُهْتَرٌ

مُهْتَرٌ Disordered in his intellect, (Ṣ,) or having lost his reason, (AZ, AʼObeyd, Ḳ,) from old age. (AZ, AʼObeyd, Ṣ, Ḳ,) or from disease, or grief: (Ḳ:) if from أَهْتَرَ, it is anomalous. (Ḳ, TA,) like مُحْصَنٌ, &c. (TA.)

Root: هتر - Entry: مُهْتَرٌ ― Signification: A2

† Addicted or given to, or fond of, speaking of a thing (Ḳ.) See also مُسْتَهْتَرٌ.

مُهْتِرٌ

مُهْتِرٌ Erring in his speech. (TA.)

مُسْتَهْتَرٌ

مُسْتَهْتَرٌ Much given to false, or vain, sayings, or actions: (Ḳ:) or one who says what is false or erroneous: or one who cares not what is said of him, nor how he is reviled: or † attached, or devoted, (مُسْتَهْتَرٌ,) to the world (IAth, TA)

Root: هتر - Entry: مُسْتَهْتَرٌ ― Signification: A2

مُسْتَهْتَرٌ بِشَىْءٍ ‡ Addicted to, or fond of, a thing, (Ḳ, TA,) or fascinated by it, (A,) not talking of any other thing, (TA,) nor caring what is done to him, (A, Ḳ, TA,) nor how he is reviled, (Ḳ,) having lost his reason; (A.) as alsoمُهْتَرٌ↓. (A.) You say, فُلَانٌ مُسْتَهْتَرٌ بِالشَّرَابِ ‡ Such a one is addicted to, or fond of, drink, not caring what is said of him. (Ṣ.)
