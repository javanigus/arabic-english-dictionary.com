هتك هث هثر

1. ⇒ هثّ

هَثَّ, [aor. ـُ {يَهْثُثُ},] inf. n. هَثٌّ, He mixed a thing, one part with another. (M.)

Root: هث - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

هَثَّ,, [aor. ـِ {يَهْثِثُ},] inf. n. هَثٌّ, He lied. (IAạr, Ḳ.)

R. Q. 1. ⇒ هثهث

هَثْهَثَ, inf. n. هَثْهَثَةٌ, He mixed, or confounded; like مَثْمَثَ. (TA.) هثهث أَمْرَهُ He confounded his affair. (TA.)

Root: هث - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: A2

هَثْهَثَ, inf. n. هَثْهَثَةٌ, It was mixed, or confounded. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: هث - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: A3

هَثْهَثَ, (inf. n. هَثْهَثَةٌ, Ḳ,) He (a magistrate, Ṣ) acted unjustly, injuriously, or tyrannically. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: هث - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: A4

هثهث النَّاسَ He (a magistrate) acted unjustly, injuriously, or tyrannically, towards the people. (TA.)

Root: هث - Entry: R. Q. 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

هَثْهَثَ, inf. n. هَثْهَثَةٌ, It sent forth quickly: (Ḳ:) [ex.] هَثْهَثَتِ السَّحَابَةُ بِقُطْرِهَا وَثَلْجِهَا The cloud sent forth quickly its rain and its snow. (Ṣ.)

Root: هث - Entry: R. Q. 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

هَثْهَثَ, inf. n. هَثْهَثَةٌ (and هثهاث, TA) He trod, or trampled, vehemently. (Ḳ.)

Root: هث - Entry: R. Q. 1. ― Signification: C2

It (a pasturing herd or flock) trod the fresh green pasture until it was destroyed, حتى يوتى [So in the L, app. حَتَّى يُؤُتِىَ].

هَثٌّ

هَثٌّ andهَثْهَثَةٌ↓ andهثهاث↓ A confusion of sounds, noises, or voices, in war or a raising of a loud cry, or clamour, or confused noise (M,) [App. inf. n., of which the verbs are هَثَّ and هَثْهَثَ]

هَثْهَثَةٌ

هَثْهَثَةٌ andهَثْهَاثٌ↓ A word imitative of somewhat of the speech of how who has the vitious kind of pronunciation termed لُثْغَة (TA.) See هَثٌّ

هَثْهَاثٌ

هَثْهَاثٌ Quick (Ḳ) rain (TA.)

Root: هث - Entry: هَثْهَاثٌ ― Signification: A2

هَثْهَاثٌ Confused, confounded: (Ḳ,) an epithet applied to a man. (TA.)

Root: هث - Entry: هَثْهَاثٌ ― Signification: A3

See هَثٌّ

Root: هث - Entry: هَثْهَاثٌ ― Signification: A4

And see هَثَّاثٌ and هَثْهَثَةٌ

Root: هث - Entry: هَثْهَاثٌ ― Signification: A5

هَثْهَاثٌ A town, or district, abounding with dust. (Ḳ)

هِثْهَاثٌ

هِثْهَاثٌ: see هَثٌّ.

هَثَّاثٌ

هَثَّاثٌ andهَثْهَاثٌ↓ A liar, (Ḳ.)

Root: هث - Entry: هَثَّاثٌ ― Signification: A2

Also, the latter, A man who tells unmixed lies. (TA.)
