جرم جرموق جرن

جُرْمُوقٌ

جُرْمُوقٌ [A kind of galoche;] a thing that is worn over the [kind of boot called] خُفّ; (Ṣ, Mgh, Mṣb, Ḳ;) called in Persian خَرْكَشْ: (Mgh:) or a small خُفّ (JK, TA) which is worn over the [ordinary] خُفّ: (TA:) an arabicized word; (Ṣ;) [probably from the Persian چَرْم “leather,” and مُوق, which is said by some to be arabicized, but by ISd to be a genuine Arabic word, meaning “a kind of خُفّ:” or it may be from سَرْمُوزَهْ, given as its Persian equivalent in the PṢ:] pl. جَرَامِيقُ. (Mṣb.)
