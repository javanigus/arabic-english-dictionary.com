1. ⇒ جدر

جَدَرَ, (Ḳ,) aor. ـُ {يَجْدُرُ}, (TA,) He made a جِدَار [app. here meaning a wall of enclosure]; syn. حَوَّطَ: (Ḳ:) or he built a جدار: and he founded it. (Ḥam p. 818.)

Root: جدر - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

He concealed himself by means of a جِدار [or wall]. (Th, Ḳ.)

Root: جدر - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

جُدِرَ, (A, Ḳ,) inf. n. جَدْرٌ; (TA;) and جَدَرَ, (Lḥ, Ḳ,) aor. ـُ {يَجْدُرُ}, inf. n. جَدْرٌ; (Lḥ, TA;) andجُدِّرَ↓, (Ṣ, A, Ḳ,) which last some disallow, because this form denotes repetition, and the verb signifies the having a disease that befalls but once in a man's life; (MF;) He (a man, Ṣ, or a child, A) had, or became attacked by, جُدَرِىّ [or small-pox]. (Ṣ, A, Ḳ.) [And جَدَرَ الجُدَرِىُّ The small-pox came forth, or broke out; as in the TḲ: for its inf. n.] جَدْرٌ signifies the coming forth, or breaking out, of the جُدَرِىّ. (Ḳ.)

Root: جدر - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: D

جَدُرَ, aor. ـُ {يَجْدُرُ}, inf. n. جَدَارَةٌ, He, or it, was, or became, adapted, disposed, apt, meet, suited, suitable, fitted, fit, competent, proper, or worthy. (Ḳ.) You say, جَدُرَ بِهِ [and لَهُ] He was, or became, adapted, disposed, apt,, &c., for it. (A.) [And جَدُرَ أَنْ يَفْعَلَ كَذَا He was, or became, adapted, disposed, apt,, &c., for doing such a thing. See جَدِيرٌ.]

Root: جدر - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: E

جَدَرَهُ He made, or called, (جَعَلَ,) him, or it, adapted, disposed, apt, meet, suited, suitable, fitted, fit, competent, proper, or worthy. (Ḳ.)

4. ⇒ اجدر

مَا أَجْدَرَهُ بِالخَيْرِ [and لِلْخَيْرِ] How well adapted or disposed, or how apt, meet, suited, suitable, fitted, fit, competent, or proper, is he for what is good! or how worthy is he of what is good! (A.) And مَا أَجْدَرَهُ أَنْ يَفْعَلَ ذٰلِكَ, and أَجْدِرْ بِهِ, How well adapted or disposed, or how apt, meet,, &c., is he for doing that! or how worthy is he to do that! (TA.) The usage of جَدُرَ, signifying “he was, or became, adapted, &c.,” refutes the assertion of certain grammarians that these two forms of the verb deviate from general rule. (MF.)

8. ⇒ اجتدر

اجتدر بِنَآءَهُ; andجدّرهُ↓, inf. n. تَجْدِيرٌ (Ḳ) and مُجَدَّرٌ; (TA;) He raised his building high; or constructed it firmly and strongly, and raised it high; syn. شَيَّدَهُ. (Ḳ, TA.) [In the CK, we read اِجتَدَرَ بِنَاهُ, as though the pronoun ه referred to the word جِدَار, which precedes; and thus the verb signified “he built a wall;” but it is shown in the TA that the right reading is that given above.]

جَدْرٌ

جَدْرٌ A wall; or a wall of enclosure; syn. حَائِطٌ; as alsoجِدَارٌ↓, [which is the more common]: (Ṣ, A, Mṣb, Ḳ:) pl. of the former, جُدُرٌ, (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ,) sometimes used as a pl. of pauc., (Sb, TA,) and جُدْرٌ; (Ḳ;) and of the latter, جُدْرَانٌ. (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ.)

Root: جدر - Entry: جَدْرٌ ― Signification: A2

The basis, or foundation, of a wall: (Ḳ:) and the side of a wall: (Lḥ, Ḳ:) pl., in both these senses, جُدُورٌ. (TA.) الجَدْرُ is applied to The [wall called the] حَطِيم (A, Ḳ) of the Kaabeh; (Ḳ;) because in it is a part of the [original] foundations of the house: (TA:) and it is also called الحِجْرُ. (A.)

Root: جدر - Entry: جَدْرٌ ― Signification: A3

† A fence, or dam, raised of branches, to retain water; likened to a wall: (Az, Mṣb:) or a fence, or dam, to confine water: pl. جُدُورٌ: (Suh, Mṣb:) and جُدُرٌ, [which is also a pl.,] signifies fences, or dams, between houses, which retain water. (TA.)

Root: جدر - Entry: جَدْرٌ ― Signification: A4

[The pl.] جُدُورٌ also signifies Gardens, or walled gardens, (حَوَائِط,) of grapes. (TA.)

جُدَرِىٌّ and جَدَرِىٌّ (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ) [Small-pox;] certain pustules (Mṣb, Ḳ) in the body, (Ḳ,) which break forth (Mṣb, Ḳ) from the skin, full of water, and afterwards opening, (Mṣb,) and generating thick purulent matter; (Ḳ;) a well-known disease, that attacks people once during life. (TA.)
