امت امد امر

1. ⇒ أمد

أَمِدَ عَلَيْهِ, aor. ـَ {يَأْمَدُ}, inf. n. أَمَدٌ, He was angry with him: (Ṣ, M, Mṣb,* Ḳ:) like أَبِدَ (Ṣ) and وَمَد and وَبِدَ and عَبِدَ. (T in art. ابد.)

2. ⇒ أمّد

أمّد, inf. n. تَأْمِيدٌ, He declared the time, considered with regard to its end; or the utmost, or extreme, extent, term, limit, point, or reach; expl. by بَيَّنَ الأَمَدَ. (Ḳ.)

أَمَدٌ

أَمَدٌ Time, considered with regard to its end: زَمَانٌ being time considered with regard to its end and its beginning: (Er-Rághib:) [but sometimes it is interchangeable with زَمَانٌ, as will be seen in what follows:] or the utmost, or extreme, extent, term, limit, point, or reach. (Ṣ, M, A, Mṣb, Ḳ.) You say, بَلَغَ أَمَدَهُ He, or it, reached, or attained, his, or its, utmost, or extreme, extent, term,, &c. (Mṣb.) And ضَرَبَ لَهُ أَمَدًا [He assigned, or appointed, for him, or it, a term, or limit]. (A.) And هُوَ بَعِيدُ الآمَادِ [He is one whose limits are remote: آمَادٌ being the pl.]. (A.)

Root: امد - Entry: أَمَدٌ ― Signification: A2

The period of life which one has reached; as in the saying, مَا أَمَدُكَ What is thy period of life which thou hast reached? (Ṣ.)

Root: امد - Entry: أَمَدٌ ― Signification: A3

Each of the two terms of the life of a man; i. e. the time of his birth, and the time of his death. (Sh, T.) El-Ḥasan [El-Basree], being asked by El-Hajjáj, مَا أَمَدُكَ, meaning What was the time of thy birth? answered by saying that it was two years before the expiration of ʼOmar's reign as Khaleefeh. (T, L, from a trad.)

Root: امد - Entry: أَمَدٌ ― Signification: A4

The startingplace, and the goal, of horses in a race. (Sh, T, L.)

Root: امد - Entry: أَمَدٌ ― Signification: A5

‡ Any space of time: (Er-Rághib:) a space of time of unknown limit. (Kull pp. 9 and 10.)

Root: امد - Entry: أَمَدٌ ― Signification: A6

Sometimes, † A particular time; as in the phrase أَمَدُ كَذَا The time of such a thing; like زَمَانُ أَمَدٌ. (Kull p. 10.)

Root: امد - Entry: أَمَدٌ ― Signification: A7

[It is also used for ذو أَمَدٌ, and (applied to a fem. n.) ذَات أَمَدٍ, Having a term, or limit; limited in duration; as in the saying,] الدُّنْيَا أَمَدٌ والآخِرةُ أَبَدٌ [The present state of existence is limited in duration, but the final state of existence is everlasting]. (ʼObeyd Ibn-ʼOmeyr, L in art. ابد.)

أُمْدَةٌ

أُمْدَةٌ A remainder, or what remains, (Ḳ,) of anything. (TA)

مُؤَمَّدٌ

سِقَآءٌ مُؤَمَّدٌ A skin [exhausted;] in which there remains not a gulp, or as much as is swallowed at once, of water. (Ḳ.)

مَأْمُودٌ

أَمَدٌ مَأْمُودٌ An extreme term, limit, or point, reached, or attained. (Ḳ.)
