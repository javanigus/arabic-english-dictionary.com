دوأ دوج دوح

1. ⇒ دوج ⇒ داج

دَاجَ, (IAạr, Ḳ,) aor. يَدُوجُ, (IAạr,) inf. n. دَوْجٌ, He (a man, IAạr) served, or did service. (IAạr, Ḳ.)

دَاجَةٌ

دَاجَةٌ [as pl. of دَائِجٌ, agreeably with analogy,] The followers of an army. (Ḳ.)

Root: دوج - Entry: دَاجَةٌ ＝ Dissociation: B

Also A small want: (Ḳ:) or a want [absolutely]; i. q. حَاجَةٌ, as some say: (TA:) or it is an imitative sequent to حَاجَةٌ. (Ḳ, and Ṣ in art. دج. [See دَاجٌّ in that art.]) It is said in a trad., مَا تَرَكْتُ مِنْ حَاجَةٍ وَلَا دَاجَةٍ [I left not a want nor a small want: or a want of any kind: or] this means I left not any act of disobedience to which I was enticed. (TA voce حَاجَةٌ.)
