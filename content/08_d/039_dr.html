دُرْدُرٌ The part of the gums where the teeth grow: (TA:) or the part where the teeth grow of a child: (Ṣ, Ḳ, TA:) or the part where the teeth grow both before they grow and after they have fallen out: (Ḳ, TA:) pl. دَرَادِرُ. (Ṣ.) Hence the prov., أَعْيَيْتِنِى بِأُشُرٍ فَكَيْفَ بِدُرْدُرٍ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) or فَكَيْفَ أَرْجُوكَ بِدُرْدُرٍ, (TA,) i. e. Thou [weariedst me, and] didst not accept good advice when thou wast a young woman and when thy teeth were serrated and sharp in their extremities; then how should I hope for any good in thee now when thou hast grown old, and the places of the growth of thy teeth have become apparent by reason of age?
