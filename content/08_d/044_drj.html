1. ⇒ درج

دَرَجَ, (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ,) aor. ـُ {يَدْرُجُ}, (Ṣ, Mṣb,) inf. n. دُرُوجٌ (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ) and دَرَجَانٌ, (Ḳ,) said of a man, and of a [lizard of the kind called] ضَبّ, (Ṣ,) He went on foot; [went step by step; stepped along;] or walked: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) and said of a child, he walked a little, at his first beginning to walk: (Mṣb, TA:*) or, said of an old man, and of a child, and of a bird of the kind called قَطًا, aor. as above, inf. n. [دُرُوجٌ and] دَرْجٌ and دَرَجَانٌ and دَرِيجٌ, he walked with a weak gait; crept along; or went, or walked, leisurely, slowly, softly, or gently. (TA.)

Root: درج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

[Hence,] دَرَجَ قَرْنٌ بَعْدَ قَرْنٍ Generation after generation passed away. (A.) And دَرَجَ القَوْمُ The people passed away, or perished, none of them remaining; (Ṣ, A, Ḳ;) as alsoاندرجوا↓. (Ṣ, Ḳ.) (Ṣ.) Or دَرَجَ signifies, (Ḳ,) or signifies also, (Ṣ,) He went his way; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) and so دَرِجَ, [aor. ـَ {يَدْرَجُ},] like سَمِعَ. (Ḳ.) لَيْسَ هٰذَا بِعُشِّكِ فَٱدْرُجِى, i. e. [This is not thy nest, therefore] go thou away, is a saying occurring in a خُطْبَة of El-Hajjáj, addressed to him who applies himself to a thing not of his business to do; or to him who is at ease in an improper time; wherefore he is thus ordered to be diligent and in motion.
