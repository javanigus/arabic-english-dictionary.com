مل ملأ ملب

1. ⇒ ملأ

مَلَأَ, aor. ـَ {يَمْلَأُ}, inf. n. مَلْءٌ (Ṣ, Ḳ) and مَلْأَةٌ and مِلْأَةٌ; (Ḳ;) and مَلِئَ; (TA;) andملّأ↓, inf. n. تَمْلِئَةٌ; (Ḳ;) He filled (Ḳ;) a vessel, &c. (Ṣ, TA.) You may also say مَلَأْتُهُ مَلًا, for مَلْئًا, (TA.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

مَلَأَ العَيْنَ ‡ He satisfied [or glutted] the eye by his comeliness of aspect. (TA.) See an ex. in a verse cited voce عَقِبٌ.

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

مَلَأْتُ مِنْهُ عَيْنِى ‡ [I satisfied, or glutted, my eye by the sight of his comeliness]. (TA.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

مَلُؤَ, aor. ـُ {يَمْلُأُ}, (Ḳ,) inf. n. مَلَآءَةٌ and مَلَآءٌ; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) and مَلَأَ, aor. ـَ {يَمْلَأُ}; (Ḳ;) the former is that which commonly obtains; (TA;) He became rich, wealthy, &c., syn. صَارَ مَلِيئًا. (Ḳ.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A5

كَلِمَةٌ تَمْلَأُ الفَمَ † [A word, or saying, that fills the mouth;] i. e., gross, and abominable; not allowable to be spoken; that fills the mouth so that it cannot articulate. (TA, from a trad.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A6

إِمْلَؤُوا أَفْوَاءَكُمْ مِنَ القُرْآنِ † [Fill your mouths with the Ḳur-án]. (TA.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A7

مُلِئَ رُعْبًا, and مَلُؤَ رعبا, ‡ He was filled with fright. (A.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A8

مَلَأَ ثِيَابِى ‡ He sprinkled my clothes with mud, &c. (A.) مَلَأَ رَاكِبَهُ [He (a camel) bespattered his rider with his ejected cud]. (Ṣ, Ḳ, art. زرد.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A9

مَلَأَ عِنَانَهُ † He made, or urged, his beast to run vehemently. (TA in art. عن.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A10

Root: ملأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A11

2. ⇒ ملّأ

ملّأ فُرُوجَ فَرَسِهِ He made his horse to run at the utmost rate of the pace termed حُضْر. (TA.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A2

And see 1, and 4.

3. ⇒ مالأ

مالأهُ عَلَى الأَمْرِ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) inf. n. مُمَالَأَةٌ; (Ṣ;) andمَلَأَهُ↓; (Ḳ;) but this latter the lexicologists do not hold in good repute; (TA;) He aided, or assisted, him, and conformed with him, to do the thing. (IAạr,* AZ, Ṣ, Ḳ.)

4. ⇒ املأ

املأ النَّزْعَ فِى قَوْسِهِ, (Ṣ,) and املأ فى قوسه, andملّأ↓ فى قوسه, (Ḳ,) ‡ He pulled his bow to the utmost. (Ṣ, Ḳ, TA.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A2

املأهُ ٱللّٰهُ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) inf. n. إِمْلَاءٌ, (TA,) † God affected him with the disease called مُلَآءَة. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

5. ⇒ تملّأ

تملّأ مِنَ الطَّعَامِ وَالشَّرَابِ He became full of food and drink. (Ṣ.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A2

See 8.

Root: ملأ - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A3

تملّأ غَيْظًا, andامتلأ↓, ‡ He became filled with rage. (Ṣ.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A4

تملّأ شِبَعًا, andامتلأ↓, He became filled to satiety. (TA.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A5

تملّأ He put on himself a مُلَآءَة; i. e., a covering of the kind so called. (TA.)

6. ⇒ تمالأ

تَمَالَؤُوا عَلَى الأَمْرِ They agreed, or conspired together, to do the thing: (ISk, Ṣ, Ḳ, TA:) they aided, or assisted, [and conformed with,] one another to do the thing. (TA.)

8. ⇒ امتلأ

امتلأ andتملّأ↓; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) and مَلِئَ, aor. ـَ {يَمْلَأُ}; (Ḳ;) It (a vessel, &c., TA) became full. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A2

See 5.

Root: ملأ - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A3

امتلأ شَبَابًا † [He became full of sap, or vigour, or youth, or young manhood]. (The Lexicons, &c., passim.) And امتلأ الشَّبَابُ † [The sap, or vigour, of youth, or young manhood, became full, or mantled, in a person.] (Ṣ, Ḳ, in art. غطى.) [And امتلأ, alone, He was, or became, plump.]

Root: ملأ - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A4

امتلأ عِنَانُهُ † The utmost of his power, or ability, was accomplished. (TA in art. عن.)

10. ⇒ استملأ

استملأ فِى الدَّيْنِ signifies جَعَلَ دَيْنَهُ فِى مُلَأٓءَ (CK, and a MṢ copy of the Ḳ) [app., He made wealthy persons, or honest wealthy persons, his debtors: but in one copy of the Ḳ, for مُلَأٓءَ, we find مُلَآءٍ, which affords no sense that seems admissible here: and in another, دِين seems to be put in the place of دَيْن, in both the above instances; and مَلَآءٍ in that of مُلَأٓءَ; for Golius renders the phrase استملأ فى الدين by opulentiæ studuit in religione sua: i. e., religionem suam in illa posuit: a meaning which IbrD rejects].

مِلْءٌ

مِلْءٌ [A thing sufficient in quantity, or dimensions, for the filling of a vessel, &c., or] the quantity that a vessel, &c., holds when it is filled. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

أَعْطِهِ مِلْأَهُ وَمِلْأَيْهِ وَثَلَاثَةَ أَمْلَآئِهِ Give it (i. e., the cup, TA) what will fill it; and what will twice fill it; and what will thrice fill it. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: مِلْءٌ ― Signification: A3

Root: ملأ - Entry: مِلْءٌ ― Signification: A5

مَلَأٌ

مَلَأٌ An assembly, (IAạr, Ṣ, Ḳ,) absolutely, (TA,) [whether of nobles or others]: pl. أَمْلَآءٌ. (IAạr.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: مَلَأٌ ― Signification: A2

Nobles; chiefs; princes; syn. أَشْرَافٌ and عِلْيَةٌ; (Ḳ;) principal persons; persons whose opinion is respected. (TA.) (المَلَأُ الأَعلْىَ [The most exalted princes; i. e.] the angels that are admitted near [to the presence of God]; or the archangels. TA.) See سَمعَهُ, for other explanations.

Root: ملأ - Entry: مَلَأٌ ― Signification: A3

A people of comely appearance, figure, attire, or adornment, united for some purpose or design; expl. by قَوْمٌ ذو الشَّارَةِ والتَّجَمُّعِ لِلْإِرَادَةِ: (Abu-l-Ḥasan, Ḳ:) [but this is wrong, see Beyd, ii. 247.] Thus it is of a different class from رَهَطٌ, though, like this word, a quasi-pl. n. It is an epithet in which the quality of a substantive predominates. (Abu-l-Ḥasan.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: مَلَأٌ ― Signification: A4

‡ Consultation. (Ḳ.) [You say,] مَا كَانَ هٰذَا الأَمْرُ عَنْ مَلَإٍ مِنَّا ‡ This thing was not the result of a consultation and consent on our part: [and] أَكَانَ هٰذَا عَنْ مَلَإٍ مِنْكُمْ ‡ Was this the result of a consultation of your nobles, and of your assembly? said by ʼOmar when he was stabbed: asserted to be tropical in this sense by Z and others. (TA.) تَحَدَّثُوا مَلَأً They conversed, consulting together. (Ṣ.)

Root: ملأ - Entry: مَلَأٌ ― Signification: A5

Opinion. (Ḳ.) [See a supposed example below.]

Root: ملأ - Entry: مَلَأٌ ― Signification: A6

Disposition; nature; manners; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) a nature rich in needful qualities: (T:) pl. أَمْلَآءٌ. (Ṣ.) [You say,] مَا أَحْسَنَ مَلَأَ بَنِى فُلَانٍ How good are the dispositions, or manners, and conversation, of the sons of such a one! (Ṣ.) El-Juhanee says,

* تَنَادَوْا يَالَ بُهْثَةَ إِذْ رَأَوْنَا *
* فَقُلْنَا أَحْسِنِى مَلَأً جُهَيْنَا *

(Ṣ) [They called out, one to another, O Buhtheh! come to our aid! when they saw us: and we said,] Be of good disposition, or manners, O Juheyneh! or, accord. to some, Be of good opinion, O Juheyneh! (see above:) or, as some say, Aid well, O Juheyneh! taking ملأ in the sense of مُمَالَأَةً: [see 3]. (TA.)

مَلِىْءٌ

مَلِىْءٌ, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) also written and pronounced مَلِىٌّ, (Nh,) A rich, wealthy, opulent, man: (Ḳ:) or trusty, or honest: (Ṣ:) or trusty, or honest, and rich: (TA:) or a rich man, or one not literally rich, who is honest, and pays his debts well, without giving trouble to his creditor: (Ḳ,* TA:) or an able, rich, man: (Mṣb:) [a solvent man:] pl. مِلَآءٌ and أَمْلِئَآءُ and مُلَأٓءُ.
