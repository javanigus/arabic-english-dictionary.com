مكو مل ملأ

1. ⇒ ملّ

مَلَّهُ He put it (namely bread, or flesh-meat,) into hot ashes, [to bake, or roast]. (Ḳ, &c.)

Root: مل - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

مَلَّ الثَّوْبَ He sewed, or tacked, the garment, or piece of cloth, [slightly,] previously to the [stronger] sewing termed الكَفّ. (Ṣ.) See also شَلَّ.

Root: مل - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

مَلِلْتُهُ, & مَلِلْتُ عَنْهُ, I was avers from it; (T;) loathed it; was disgusted by it, with it, or at it; (T, Ṣ, Ḳ;) [was weary of it;] turned away from it with disgust. (T.) See سَئِمَ.

Root: مل - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

مَلَّ مَلاَلُكَ [May thy disgust pass away, or cease]: see أَضَلَّ ٱللّٰهُ ضَلَالَكَ.

4. ⇒ املّ

أَمْلَلْتُ & أَمْلَيْتُ: see 4 in art. حظ.

Root: مل - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A2

أَمَلَّ: see an ex. voce أَدَلَّ.

8. ⇒ امتلّ

اِمْتَلَّ مِلَّتَهُ He follows his way of religion: see 8 in art. شرع.

مَلَّةٌ

مَلَّةٌ The hollow that is made for baking bread: or the hot dust and ashes [in which the bread is baked]. (Mṣb.)

Root: مل - Entry: مَلَّةٌ ― Signification: A2

Hot ashes: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) ashes, and earth, in which fire is kindled. (TA, art. خبز.)

Root: مل - Entry: مَلَّةٌ ― Signification: A3

خُبْزُ مَلَّةٍ Bread baked in hot ashes. (Ṣ.) [It is generally made in the form of thick round cakes.]

مِلَّةٌ

مِلَّةٌ A religion; (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ;) a way of belief and practice in respect of religion. (T, &c.)

Root: مل - Entry: مِلَّةٌ ― Signification: A2

See 8.

مَلُولٌ

مَلُولٌ Conceiving [frequent] disgust. (Mṣb.) See ذَوَّاقٌ.

مَلاَلٌ

مَلاَلٌ: see 1.

مَلِيلٌ

مَلِيلٌ A man burned by the sun; as alsoمَمْلُولٌ↓. (TA.) See an ex. in a verse cited voce أَصْرَمُ; and see طُلْمَهٌ.

مَمْلُولٌ

مَمْلُولٌ Flesh-meat covered over in live coals. (TA, art. عرض.)

Root: مل - Entry: مَمْلُولٌ ― Signification: A2

See مَلِيلٌ.

مُلْمُولٌ

مُلْمُولٌ An iron style with which one writes on tablets. (Ḳ.)

Root: مل - Entry: مُلْمُولٌ ― Signification: A2

The style, or bodkin, with which collyrium is applied to the eyes. (Ṣ, Ḳ.) In the CK, incorrectly, مَلْمُولٌ: the former is found in MṢ. copies of the Ḳ, as well as in the Ṣ, and is right accord. to the TḲ.
