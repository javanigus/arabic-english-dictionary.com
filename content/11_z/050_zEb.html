زع زعب زعج

1. ⇒ زعب

زَعَبْتُهُ عَنِّى, [aor. ـَ {يَزْعَبُ},] inf. n. زَعْبٌ, I pushed, thrust, drove away, or repelled, him, or it, from me. (Ṣ, TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

[Hence,] زَعَبَ said of a torrent, aor. and inf. n. as above, It was impelled in its several parts, or portions, by the impetus of one part, or portion, acting upon another; in a valley: (Ṣ, TA:) [it was, or became, copious, and drove along, one portion impelling another; likeاِزْلَعَبَّ↓:] it ran. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

If you say رَعَبَ with the unpointed ر, (Ṣ, TA.) or thus and also زَعَبَ, aor. and inf. n. as above, (TA,) you mean It filled the valley. (Ṣ, TA.) And the latter, It filled everything: said of rain. (TA.) And He filled a vessel. (Ḳ, TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

[Hence,] زَعَبَ المَرْأَةَ, (Ḳ, TA,) aor. and inf. n. as above, is a tropical phrase, meaning جَامَعَهَا فَمَلَأَ فَرْجَهَا بِفَرْجِهِ: (TA:) or جَامَعَهَا فَمَلَأَ فَرْجَهَا مَنِيًّا: (Ḳ,* TA:) [neither of which explanations needs translation:] but, accord. to some, this is said only of one that is large, or bulky. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A5

زَعَبَ القِرْبَةَ He took up and carried the water-skin, it being full: (Ḳ, TA:) he carried it filled; as also زَأَبَهَا: (TA:) and both signify he carried it in his bosom: (TA in art. زأب: [see also 8:]) the former also signifies he was impelled onwards (تدافع) by it, carrying it, by reason of its weight. (TA in the present art.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A6

زَعَبَ بِحِمْلِهِ, said of a camel, (Ḳ, TA,) i. q. اِسْتَقَامَ [app. meaning He went right on, straight on, or undeviatingly, with his load]: (TA:) or, as alsoازدعب↓ بِهِ, he went along with his load oppressed by its weight: or he was impelled onwards (تَدَافَعَ) by it: (Ḳ, TA:) or مَرَّ يَزْعَبُ بِهِ he went along quickly with it: or he went along easily with it; namely, his load. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A7

زَعَبَ الوَادِى The valley became filled, (Ḳ, TA,) so that the several parts, or portions, of the torrent impelled one another. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A8

زَعَبَ فِى قَيْئِهِ He vomited much, so that one portion [of the vomit] impelled on another. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A9

زَعَبَتِ القِرْبَةُ i. q. دفعت مَآءَهَا [The water-skin propelled its water: or the right reading is probably دَفَقَتْ مَآءَهَا, i. e., poured forth its water; or poured it forth with vehemence]. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A10

زَعَبَ also signifies He cut off, or divided off; and soازدعب↓. (Ḳ, TA.) You say, زَعَبَ لَهُ مِنَ المَالِ, andازدعب↓, as also ازدهب, He cut off, or divided off, for him [a portion] of the property, or wealth. (TA.) And زَعَبْتُ لَهُ زُعْبَةً مِنَ المَالِ, and زَعْبَةً, (Ṣ, Ḳ,*) and زِعْبًا, (Ḳ,) I gave to him a part, or portion, of the property, or wealth: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) or a full, or an ample, or abundant, portion thereof. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A11

زَعَبَ الشَّرَابَ, aor. and inf. n. as above, He drank all the wine, or beverage. (TA.)

5. ⇒ تزعّب

تزعّب He was brisk, lively, or sprightly; (Ḳ, TA;) and quick. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A2

He was, or became, angered, or enraged. (Ḳ.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A3

تزعّب فِى أَكْلِهِ وَشُرْبِهِ He was immoderate in his eating and his drinking; he ate and drank much. (Ḳ.)

Root: زعب - Entry: 5. ＝ Dissociation: B

تزعّب القَوْمُ المَالَ The people, or party, divided among themselves the property, or wealth; (Ḳ;) divided it into parts, or portions. (TA.)

8. ⇒ ازتعب ⇒ ازدعب

اِزْدَعَبْتُ الشَّىْءَ I carried the thing; took it up and carried it; or carried it off or away; syn. حَمَلْتُهُ: you say, مَرَّ بِهِ فَٱزْدَعَبَهُ [He passed by it and carried it off or away]. (Ṣ.) [See also ازدغب.]

Root: زعب - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A2

See also 1, in three places.

Q. Q. 4. ⇒ اِزْلَعَبَّ

اِزْلَعَبَّ: see 1, second sentence; and see also art. زلعب.

زَعْبٌ

زَعْبٌ Muchness, copiousness, abundance, or a large quantity or number. (TA.)

زِعْبٌ

زِعْبٌ: see what next follows.

زَعْبَةٌ: see what next follows.

زُعْبَةٌ

زُعْبَةٌ andزَعْبَةٌ↓ A part, or portion, that is given, of property, or wealth; (Ṣ, Ḳ;*) as alsoزِعْبٌ↓: (Ḳ:) or a full, or an ample, or abundant, portion, that is given, thereof; and so زُهْبَةٌ. (TA.) [See 1, last sentence but one.]

زَعُوبٌ

زَعُوبٌ: see what next follows, in two places.

زَاعِبٌ

زَاعِبٌ A torrent driving along, one portion of it impelling another; (A, TA;) as alsoزَعُوبٌ↓ [but app. in an intensive sense; andمُزْلَعِبٌّ↓, also, has the same, or a similar, meaning]: or the first and↓ second signify a torrent filling the valley: and the first is also applied to rain, as meaning filling everything. (TA.)

Root: زعب - Entry: زَاعِبٌ ― Signification: A2

Also A guide of the way, one who is a frequent traveller. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

زَاعِبِىٌّ

زُمْحٌ زَاعِبِىٌّ, (A,) and رِمَاحٌ زَاعِبِيَّةٌ, (A, Ḳ,) A spear, (A,) and spears, (A, Ḳ,) so called in relation to a certain man named Zá'ib (زَاعِبٌ), (A, Ḳ,) of El-Khazraj, who made spear-heads: (A:) so says Mbr: (TA:) or † the spears thus termed are such as quiver much (عَسَّالَةٌ), which, when shaken, have a motion like the torrent termed زَاعِبٌ, of which one portion impels another: the ى being that of the rel. n.; to give intensiveness to the signification, as in أَحْمَرِىٌّ: (A:) or ‡ such that, when they are shaken, their كُعُوب [i. e. knots, or joints, or their internodal portions,] are as though they ran one into another, (Ḳ, TA,) by reason of its pliancy: so says Aṣ: and this meaning is tropical; for it is from the phrase مَرَّ يَزْعَبُ بِحِمْلِهِ meaning “he went along [impelled by his load, or] easily with his load:” accord. to another explanation, زاعبىّ applied to a spear means such that, when it is shaken, the whole of it is [in appearance] impelled in its several parts by the impetus of one part acting upon another, as though its hind part ran into its fore part: (TA:) or زَاعِبِيَّةٌ signifies spears, (Ṣ, TA,) universally. (TA.) You say also سِنَانٌ زَاعِبِىٌّ (Ṣ, TA) A spear-head of Zá'ib. (TA.)

مُزْلَعِبٌّ

مُزْلَعِبٌّ: see زَاعِبٌ; and see also art. زلعب.
