1. ⇒ سلب

Root: سلب - Entry: مُسَلِّبٌ ― Signification: A2

مَالِى أَرَاكَ مُسَلِّبًا i. e. [What hath happened to me that I see thee] unfamiliar, not inclining to any one? is a saying whereby a man is likened to a wild animal: one says also, إِنَّهُ لَوَحْشىٌّ مُسَلِّبٌ, meaning Verily he is unsociable and ungentle. (AZ, L, TA.)

مَسْلُوبٌ

مَسْلُوبٌ: see سَلِيبٌ, first sentence.

مُسْتَلَبُ

مُسْتَلَبُ العَقْلِ: see سَلِيبٌ, last sentence.

المُسْتَلِبُ

المُسْتَلِبُ the name of A sword of ʼAmr Ibn Kulthoom: and of another, belonging to Aboo-Dahbal. (Ḳ.)
