خزعبل خزف خزق

خَزَفٌ

خَزَفٌ Pottery; jars; or earthern vessels; syn. جَرٌّ; (Lth, Ṣ, Ḳ; [and so in the present day;]) and anything made of clay, and baked, so that it becomes فَخَّار: (IDrd, Ḳ:) or clay made into vessels, before it is baked; i. q. صَلْصَالٌ: when baked, it is called فَخَّار. (Mṣb.) [See an ex. in a verse cited voce إِنْ, p. 107.]

خَزَفِىٌّ

خَزَفِىٌّ [Of, or relating to, pottery, or jars,, &c.;] rel. n. of خَزَفٌ. (TA.)

Root: خزف - Entry: خَزَفِىٌّ ― Signification: A2

See also what follows.

خَزَّافٌ

خَزَّافٌ andخَزَفِىٌّ↓ A seller [or maker] of خَزَف [or pottery, jars,, &c.]. (TA.)
