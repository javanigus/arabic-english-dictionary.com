And it is said in a trad., فَيَحُفُّونَهُمْ بِأَجْنِحَتِهِمْ And they circuit round about them with their wings. (TA.) And in a prov., مَنْ حَفَّنَا أَوْ رَفَّنَا فَلْيَقْتَصِدْ, i. e. Whoso goes round about us, and minds, or manages, our affairs, (Ḳ, TA,) and treats us with honour; (TA;) or [in the Ḳ “and”] serves us, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and guards us, defends us, or takes care of us, and regards us, or behaves towards us, with benevolence and solicitude; (Ṣ, TA;) or [in the Ḳ “and”] praises us; (AʼObeyd, Ḳ, TA;) let him [act moderately, and] not exceed the due bounds, (AʼObeyd, Ḳ, TA,) but speak truth. It is said in the Ḳur [xxxix. last verse], وَتَرَى المَلَائِكَةَ حَافِّينَ مِنْ حَوْلِ العَرْشِ (Zj, Ṣ, Ḳ *) And thou shalt see the angels surrounding the عرش: (Zj, TA:) or surrounding the sides thereof: (Ṣgh, Ḳ:) or going round about on either side thereof. (Er-Rághib, TA.)

مِحَفَّةٌ

مِحَفَّةٌ, with kesr; (Ṣ, Ṣgh, Mṣb, Ḳ;) in the “Mesháriḳ” of ʼIyáḍ said to be [مَحَفَّةٌ,] with fet-ḥ, (MF,) A vehicle of the kind used for women, like the هَوْدَج, (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ,) except that it has no قُبَّة [or dome-like, or tent-like, top], (Ṣ, Ḳ,) which the هودج has: (Ṣ:) or a camel's saddle (رَحْل) surrounded (يُحَفُّ [with pieces of cloth (see 1) upon a wooden frame]), upon which a woman rides: accord. to IDrd, so called because the [frame of] wood [with the pieces of cloth attached thereto] surrounds on all sides the sitter upon it. (TA.)
