طَبَّ, aor. ـُ {يَطْبُبُ}, (O, Mṣb, Ḳ, TA,) agreeably with analogy in the case of a trans. reduplicative verb [like this], (TA,) and ـِ, (Ḳ, TA,) which is irregular, (TA,) inf. n. طَبٌّ (O,* Mṣb, Ḳ,* TA) [and app. طِبٌّ and طُبٌّ also accord. to the O and Ḳ, but, accord. to the Mṣb, طِبٌّ is the subst. from this verb, and app. طُبٌّ is likewise a simple subst.], He treated medically, therapeutically, or curatively, (O,* Mṣb, Ḳ,*) another person (O, Mṣb) [or the body, and in like manner the soul: see طِبٌّ, below].

طَبْطَابَةٌ, or طِبْطَابَةٌ, (accord. to different copies of the Ḳ,) A broad piece of wood, with which one plays with the ball, (Ḳ, TA,) or with which the horseman plays with the ball. (T, TA.)

الطَّبَاطِبُ

الطَّبَاطِبُ The عَجَم [i. e. Persians, or foreigners]. (L, TA.)

أَطَبُّ / أَطَبُّونَ

أَطَبُّ; and its pl. أَطَبُّونَ: see طَبٌّ.

مَطْبُوبٌ

مَطْبُوبٌ ‡ A man enchanted, or fascinated. (Ṣ, A.)

مُتَطَبِّبٌ

مُتَطَبِّبٌ: see طَبِيبٌ.
