طنجر طنخ طنز

1. ⇒ طنخ

طَنِخَ, (Ṣ, L, Ḳ,) aor. ـَ {يَطْنَخُ}, (Ḳ,) inf. n. طَنَخٌ, (Ṣ,) His (a man's, Ṣ) heart (قَلْب [meaning stomach, which is often thus termed in the present day,]) became overpowered by grease [or greasy food], and he suffered indigestion, (Ṣ, L, Ḳ,) in consequence thereof; (Ṣ, L;) as also تَنِخَ, aor. ـَ {يَطْنَخُ}, inf. n. تَنَخٌ. (L.) And طَنِخَتْ said of camels, They suffered indigestion. (TA in art. طنح.) And طَنِخَتْ نَفْسُهُ His soul [or stomach] became heavy; or heaved, or became agitated by a tendency to vomit. (L.)

Root: طنخ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

And He became fat. (L, Ḳ.)

2. ⇒ طنّخ

طنّخهُ, (Ḳ,) inf. n. تَطْنِيخٌ; (TA;) andاطنخهُ↓, (Ḳ,) inf. n. إِطْنَاخٌ; (TA;) It (grease [or greasy food], TA) caused him to suffer indigestion. (Ḳ, TA.)

Root: طنخ - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A2

Sh says, I heard El-Fak'asee say, نَشْرَبُ هٰذِهِ الأَلْبَانَ فتطنخنا عَنِ الطَّعَامِ [i. e. فَتُطَنِّخُنَا orفَتُطْنِخُنَا↓] meaning [We drink these milks and] they render us in no need, or serve us in stead, of [other] food. (L.)

Root: طنخ - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A3

And one says, طُنِّخَتِ النَّاقَةُ, and الدَّابَّة, meaning The she-camel, and the beast, became [or was rendered] very fat. (L.)

4. ⇒ اطنخ

see 2, in two places.

طِنْخٌ

طِنْخٌ [is said to signify] A part, or portion, of the night: so in the saying, مَرَّ طِنْخٌ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ [A part, or portion, of the night passed]: (Ḳ:) but IDrd doubted its correctness. (TA.)

طَنِخٌ

طَنِخٌ A man whose heart [or stomach] is overpowered by grease [or greasy food], and who suffers indigestion in consequence thereof; as alsoطَانِخٌ↓. (L.) [See also 1, of which each is a part. n.]

طَنَخَةٌ

طَنَخَةٌ; expl. in the Ḳ [and in the JK, app. from the ʼEyn,] as syn. with أَحْمَقُ, is a mistranscription, correctly طيخة [i. e. طَيْخَةٌ]. (TA.)

طَانِخٌ

طَانِخٌ: see طَنِخٌ.
