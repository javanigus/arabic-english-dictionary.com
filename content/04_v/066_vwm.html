ثول ثوم ثوى

ثُومٌ / ثُومَةٌ

ثُومٌ [Garlic; the allium sativum of Linn.;] a kind of بَقْل, (AḤn, M,) well known, (Ṣ,) abundant in the country of the Arabs; (AḤn, M;) of two sorts; wild, (AḤn, M, Ḳ,) and growing in the cultivated tracts, (AḤn, M,) or in gardens: (Ḳ:) the former sort is called ثُومُ الحَيَّةِ, and is the stronger, (Ḳ,) and is brought from Syria: (TA:) each of them is heating, expels flatulence and worms, and is strongly diuretic; and this is the most excellent [property] that is therein: it is good for obliviousness, and asthma, and chronic cough, and [pain in] the spleen and the flank, and colic, and sciatica,, &c.: (Ḳ: [in which are added many other supposed uses:]) n. un. with ة {ثُومَةٌ}. (AḤn, M, Ḳ.)

Root: ثوم - Entry: ثُومٌ ― Signification: A2

Hence, as being likened thereto, (M,) ثُومَةٌ signifies also ‡ The pommel of a sword. (Ṣ, M, Ḳ.) Whence, أُمُّ ثُوْمَةَ, said to be the name of a certain woman, may mean ‡ A sword. (M.)

Root: ثوم - Entry: ثُومٌ ― Signification: A3

[Hence, also,] ثُوْمَةُ ذَكَرِ رَجُلٍ † [The glans of a man's penis]. (Az, in TA voce طُرْثُوثٌ.)

Root: ثوم - Entry: ثُومٌ ― Signification: A4

الثُّومَةُ also signifies The channel [or oblong depression] between the two mustaches, against the partition between the two nostrils; (IAạr, TA;) i. q. الخُنْعُبَةُ, &c. (TA in art. خنعب.)

Root: ثوم - Entry: ثُومٌ ＝ Dissociation: B

ثُومٌ is also a dial. var. of فُومٌ, meaning Wheat. (Lḥ, T, M.) And the latter is used in the [ordinary] sense of the former. (T.)
