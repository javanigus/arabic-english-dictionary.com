وثى وج وجأ

وجٌّ

وجٌّ A kind of medicine; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) certain twigs, or rods, with which one fumigates; (TA;) or which are used medicinally or remedially; (T;) [acorum, (Golius,) or acorus; sweet-cane, or, as some say, galangal: see Diosc., I. i., c. 2., referred to by Golius:] thought by El-Jawáleekee to be not pure Arabic; i. e., an arabicized word, from the Persian [وَجْ]: (TA:) so says J. (Ṣ.)
