لطأ لطث لطح

1. ⇒ لطث

لَطَثَهُ, (aor. ـِ {يَلْطِثُ}, inf. n. لَطْثٌ, L,) He struck him with the flat of the hand; or, with a broad piece of wood: (IAạr, Ḳ:) he slapped him with his open hand; syn. صَكَّهُ; (Ḳ;) like لَطَمَهُ. (TA.) [See also لَطَسَهُ.]

Root: لطث - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

لَطَثَهُ بِحَجَرٍ He threw a stone at him; (Ḳ;) as also لَطَسَهُ. (TA.)

Root: لطث - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

لَطَثَهُ He collected it together. (Ḳ.)

Root: لطث - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

لَطَثَهُ الأَمْرُ, (aor. ـِ {يَلْطِثُ}, inf. n. لَطْثٌ,) The affair was difficult, or troublesome, to him. (Ḳ.)

Root: لطث - Entry: 1. ― Signification: C2

لَطَثَهُ, aor. ـِ {يَلْطِثُ}, inf. n. لَطْثٌ, It (a load, or an affair,) was heavy or burdensome, and hard, or grievous to him. (L.)

Root: لطث - Entry: 1. ― Signification: C3

لَطَثٌ, [aor. ـُ {يَلْطُثُ},] inf. n. لَطْثٌ, It became corrupt. (IAạr, Ḳ.)

6. ⇒ تلاطث

تَلَاطَثَ المَوْجُ The waves dashed together, or against each other. (Ḳ)

Root: لطث - Entry: 6. ― Signification: A2

تلاطت القَوْمُ The people struck each other with their hands: (Ḳ:) or, with swords. (TA.)

مَلَاطِتُ

مَلَاطِتُ Places that are struck (تُلْطَثُ) by a load, or burden, or by beating: (Ḳ:) a quasi-pl. n., or a pl. without a sing., or having a pl. respecting which the lexicologists do not agree. (MF.)

مُلَاطِثٌ

مُلَاطِثٌ Collecting; or a collector; syn. جَامِعٌ: (Ḳ:) selling; or a seller; or buying; or a buyer; syn. بَائِعٌ. (AA.)
