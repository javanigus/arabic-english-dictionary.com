قم قمأ قمح

1. ⇒ قمأ

قَمَأَتِ المَاشِيَةُ, aor. ـَ {يَقْمَأُ}; (AZ, Ṣ, O, Ḳ;) and قَمُؤَت, (O, Ḳ,) aor. ـُ {يَقْمُأُ}; (Ḳ;) inf. n. قُمُوْءٌ and قُمُوءَةٌ, (AZ, Ṣ, O, Ḳ) both of the former verb, (AZ, Ṣ, O,) and قَمْءٌ (Ḳ) and قَمَآءَةٌ, (O, Ḳ,) which is of the latter verb, (O, TA,) and قَمَآءٌ, (Ḳ,) also of the latter verb; (TA;) The cattle became fat, or plump; (AZ, Ṣ, O, Ḳ;) as alsoاقمأت↓: (Ḳ:) the first is expl. in the T as meaning the cattle became full with fatness: and the epithet applied to them is there said to be قَامِئَةٌ↓. (TA.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

And قَمَأَتِ الإِبِلُ بِالمَكَانِ, (Ḳ, TA,) and قَمُؤَت, (Ḳ,) The camels abode in the place, (Ḳ, TA,) and were pleased with it, (TA,) because of its abundant pasture, and became fat, or plump, (Ḳ, TA,) in it. (TA.) And قَمَأَتِ المَاشِيَةُ مَكَانَ كَذَا حَتَّى سَمِنَتْ [The cattle abode in such a place until they became fat, or plump]. (TA.) See also 5.

Root: قمأ - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

And قَمَأْتُ بِالمَكَانِ, (O, TA,) inf. n. قَمْءٌ, (TA,) I abode in the place: (O:) or I entered the place and abode in it. (TA.) And قَمَأَ إِلَى مَنْزِلٍ He went into an abode. (TA.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

قَمَأَهُ is also syn. with قَمَعَهُ [q. v.]: (Ḳ:) the latter is affirmed to be the original word: (MF:) you say قَمَأْتُ الرَّجُلَ, meaning قَمَعْتُهُ. (O.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

قَمُؤَ, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ,) aor. ـُ {يَقْمُأُ} and قَمَأَ, aor. ـَ {يَقْمَأُ}; (Ḳ;) inf. n. قَمَآءَةٌ (Ṣ, O, Ḳ) and قَمَآءٌ, both of the former verb, (Ṣ, O,) and قُمْءٌ and قِمْءٌ, (so in copies of the Ḳ,) or قُمْأَةٌ and قِمْأَةٌ, (so in the TḲ,) and قَمْأَةٌ, (Ḳ,) which last is not an inf. n. un., (L, TA,) said of a man, (Ṣ, O, TA,) and of other than a man, (TA,) He was, or became, little and despicable (Ṣ, O, Ḳ, TA) in the eyes [of others]: (TA:) the former verb is the better known in this sense. (MF, TA.)

3. ⇒ قامأ

مَا قَامَأَهُ It (a thing, TA) did not suit him: (Ḳ, TA:) and so ما قَانَأَهُ. (TA.)

4. ⇒ اقمأ

اقمأت المَاشِيَةُ: see 1, first sentence.

Root: قمأ - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A2

اقمأ القَوْمُ The people, or party, had their camels in a fat, or plump, state. (Ṣ, O, Ḳ.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 4. ― Signification: A3

اقمأ المَرْعَى الإِبِلَ The pasture, or place of pasture, suited the camels, (Ḳ, TA,) and rendered them fat, or plump. (TA, as from the Ḳ.) And اقمأهُ It (a thing, Ṣ, O, or a place, or pasture or a place of pasture, TA) pleased him. (Ṣ, O, Ḳ, TA.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 4. ＝ Dissociation: B

And اقمأهُ He rendered him little and despicable. (Ṣ, O, Ḳ.)

5. ⇒ تقمّأ

تقمّأَ المَكَانَ (in the CK المكانُ) [He found that] the place suited him, and consequently he abode in it. (O, Ḳ.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A2

تقمّأ الشَّىْءِ He took the best of the thing. (Th, Ḳ.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A3

And He collected the thing little by little: (Ṣ, O:) and accord. to Z,اقتمأ↓ الشَّىْءِ signifies He collected the thing. (TA.)

8. ⇒ اقتمأ

see what immediately precedes {5}.

قَمْءٌ

قَمْءٌ A place in which a she-camel, and a he-camel, and a woman, and a man, abides until she, or he, becomes fat, or plump. (TA.)

قَمْأَةٌ

قَمْأَةٌ Abundance of herbage, or of the goods, conveniences, and comforts, of life; and ease, repose, or freedom from trouble or inconvenience or from toil or fatigue; as alsoقُمْأَةٌ↓. (Ḳ.)

Root: قمأ - Entry: قَمْأَةٌ ― Signification: A2

Also, andمَقْمَأَةٌ↓ andمَقْمُؤَةٌ↓, (like مقنأ [a mistranscription for مقنأة] and مقنؤة, TA,) A place on which the sun does not come: (O, Ḳ:) pl. of the first word قِمَآءٌ. (TA.)

قُمْأَةٌ

قُمْأَةٌ: see the next preceding paragraph.

قَمِىْءٌ

قَمِىْءٌ Little and despicable (Ṣ, O, Ḳ, TA) in the eyes [of others]; fem. قَمِيئَةٌ: (TA:) pl. قِمَآءٌ and قُمَآءٌ; (Ḳ;) the latter of a [very] rare form. (TA.)

قَامِئَةٌ

قَامِئَةٌ, fem. of قَامِئٌ: see 1, first sentence.

مَقْمَأةٌ / مَقْمُؤَةٌ

مَقْمَأةٌ and مَقْمُؤَةٌ: see قَمْأَةٌ.
