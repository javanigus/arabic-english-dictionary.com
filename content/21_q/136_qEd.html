1. ⇒ قعد

قَعَدَ, (Ṣ, Ḳ, &c.,) aor. ـُ {يَقْعُدُ}, (A, L,) inf. n. قُعُودٌ and مَقْعَدٌ (Ṣ, L, Ḳ) and قَعْدٌ, (L,) He sat; i. q. جَلَسَ [when the latter is used in its largest sense]; (Ṣ, A, L, Ḳ;) so accord. to 'Orweh Ibn-Zubeyr, a high authority; contr. of قَامَ: (L:) or it signifies he sat down; or sat after standing: and جلس, he sat after lying on his side or prostrating himself: (Kh, IKh, El-Ḥareeree, Ḳ:) or, as some say, قعد signifies he sat for some length of time. (MF.) See also جَلَسَ.

[And hence, He remained.]

(IAạr, L.)
