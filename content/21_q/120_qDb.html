1. ⇒ قضب

قَضَبَهُ, (Ṣ, M, A, &c.,) aor. ـِ {يَقْضِبُ}, (M, Mgh, Mṣb, Ḳ,) inf. n. قَضْبٌ, (M, Mgh, O, Mṣb,) He cut it, or cut it off; (Ṣ, M, A, Mgh, O, Mṣb, Ḳ;) as alsoاقتضبهُ↓; (M, Mṣb, Ḳ;) andقضّبهُ↓; (M, Ḳ;) [or this last is used in an intensive sense, or in relation to a number of objects:] you say, قَضَبَ الغُصْنَ [He cut off the branch]; andاقتضب↓ غُصْنًا مِنْ شَجَرَةٍ [He cut off a branch from a tree]; andقضّب↓ فُضُولَ أَغْصَانِ الشَّجَرِ [He cut off the redundant portions of the branches of the trees], inf. n. تَقْضِيبٌ. (A.)

Root: قضب - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

See also 8, in two places.

Root: قضب - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

And قضَبَهُ, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ, JM,) aor. in this case ـُ, (JM,) inf. n. قَضْبٌ, (Ṣ, TA,) He struck him, or beat him, (i. e. a man, Ḳ,) with a قضيب, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ, JM,) i. e. a rod, or stick, or the like. (TA.)

And [hence] ‡ He became cut off, or separated, from his companions. (A.)

8. ⇒ اقتضب

see 1, in two places. You say, اِقْتَضَبْتُهُ, meaning I cut it off from the thing. (Ṣ, O.)

Root: قضب - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A2

And [hence] one says, كَانَ يُحَدِّثُنَا فُلَانٌ فَجَآءَ زَيْدٌ فَٱقْتَضَبَ حَدِيثَهُ, meaning اِقْتَطَعَهُ and اِنْتَزَعَهُ [i. e. Such a one was talking to us, and Zeyd came, and broke off his talk, and turned it to what was wholly different in subject, or to what had but little connection with the subject of the former discourse: an ex. of a common conventional usage of اقتضب, mentioned in rhetorical treatises, &c.; as when a poet breaks off his نَسِيب to enter upon the main subject of his ode]. (A.)

Root: قضب - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A3

And اقتضب ‡ He extemporized, or uttered without having prepared it, (Ṣ, M, A, O,) speech, (Ṣ, A, O,) or a narrative, and poetry, or verses. (M.)

Root: قضب - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A4

And ‡ He rode (Ṣ, A, Ḳ) a beast, (Ṣ,) or a she-camel, (A,) before it, or she, was trained, or broken-in; (Ṣ, A, Ḳ;) and (Ṣ, Ḳ) so قَضَبَ↓, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ,) aor. قَضَبَ. (Ḳ.) And (TA) † He took from the camels, and trained, one in an untrained state; (M, Ḳ;) as alsoاقتضبهُ↓. (TA.) And † He rode a young camel for a night, before it was trained. (TA.)

Root: قضب - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A5

Root: قضب - Entry: 8. ― Signification: A6

And ‡ He slaughtered him, namely, a camel, in a state of freedom from disease and in a fat and youthful condition. (A.)

قَضْبَةٌ

قَضْبَةٌ Such as are cut, and eaten in their fresh state, of plants, (M, Mṣb, Ḳ,) of any kind; as is said in the Bári'; (Mṣb;) a pl. [or rather coll. gen. n.] of which the sing. [or n. un.] is قَضْبَةٌ↓: (Ḳ:) or it signifies, (Ṣ, O, Mṣb,) or signifies also, (Ḳ,) [a species of trefoil, or clover; i. e.] i. q. رَطْبَةٌ, (Ṣ, O, Mṣb,) which is the same as فِصْفِصَة, (Mṣb,) or قَتٌّ, [which is also the same,] (Ḳ,) the name by which the people of Mekkeh call قَتّ, (Fr, TA,) and (Ḳ) called in Pers. (Ṣ, O) إِسْفِسْت, (Ṣ, Mgh, Ḳ,) or إِسْبِسْت; (O;) as alsoقَضْبَةٌ↓; (Ṣ;) or this is the sing. [or rather n. un.] of قَضْبٌ, which signifies فَصَافِصُ [pl. of فِصْفِصَةٌ]; (M;) called قَضْبٌ because it is cut. (Mgh.)

Root: قضب - Entry: قَضْبَةٌ ― Signification: A3

And Certain trees from which bows are made; (En-Naḍr, O, Ḳ;) said to be of the kind called نَبْعٌ. (O.) AḤn says that قضب [accord. to the L and TA app. قَضْبٌ, but accord. to a copy of the M قَضَبٌ↓, which I think a mistranscription,] is the name of Certain trees of the plains, or soft tracts, growing among collections of [other] trees; having leaves like those of the pear-tree, except that they are thinner, and more soft; and as trees [in general] resembling pear-trees: the camels feed upon its leaves and the extremities of its branches; but when the camel has become satiated therewith, he forsakes it for a time, for it sets his teeth on edge, and irritates his chest, and occasions him cough. (M, L, TA.) (ISh, TA. [See also قَضْبَةٌ below.])
