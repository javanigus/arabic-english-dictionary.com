قطع قطف قطمر

قِطْفٌ

قِطْفٌ A bunch of grapes, &c.: pl. قُطُوفٌ: see an ex. voce ذَلَّلَ.

Root: قطف - Entry: قِطْفٌ ― Signification: A2

قِطْفٌ i. q. مَقْطُوفٌ. (TA in art. بسط.) See بَسِيطٌ.

قَطَفٌ

قَطَفٌ: see بَقْلٌ.

قَطَافٌ

قَطَافٌ andقِطَافٌ↓ The time of gathering the crop of grapes: (Ṣ, Mgh, Ḳ:) or the latter has this meaning; and the former is allowable accord. to Ks: (T, TA:) and the latter is also an inf. n., (Mgh,) or may be so, (Ks, T, TA,) meaning the gathering of the crop of grapes: (Mgh:) [or both have this meaning; for] you say, هٰذَا زَمَانُ القَطَافِ and القِطَافِ. (Mṣb.) See جَدَادٌ.

قَطِيفٌ

قَطِيفٌ a coll. gen. n. syn. with قَطَائِفُ, mentioned in the TA voce أُبْلُوجٌ, which see.

Root: قطف - Entry: قَطِيفٌ ― Signification: A2

قَطِيفَةٌ A villous, or nappy, دِثَار [or outer wrapping garment]. (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ.) See also راَحُولَاتٌ.

قَطَائِفُ

قَطَائِفُ: see my 1001 Nights, note 23 to chap. viii. See also زَلاَبِيَه. In the TA, art. كنف, it is applied to كُنَافَة.

مِقْطَفٌ

مِقْطَفٌ (vulg. مَقْطَفٌ) [pl. مَقَاطِف] A handbasket, made of palm-leaves: so called because originally used in gathering fruit. (See also قُفَّة and زَنْبِيل.)
