قَصْرٌ andقَصَرٌ↓ andقُصْرَةٌ↓ [like the inf. n. قُصُورٌ] The falling, or stopping, or coming, short of accomplishing an affair; or of doing what one ought, or is commanded, to do; or flagging, or remissness: you say to a man whom you have sent to accomplish some needful affair, and who has fallen short of doing what you commanded him to do, on account of heat or some other cause, مَا مَنَعَكَ أَنْ تَبْلُغَ المَكَانَ الَّذِى أَمَرْتُكَ بِهِ إِلَّا أَنَّكَ أَحْبَبْتَ القَصْرَ, and القَصَرَ, and القُصْرَةَ, i. e. أَنْ تُقَصِّرَ [Nothing prevented thy reaching the place to which I commanded thee to go but thy loving to fall short, &c.; or to flag, or be remiss]. (M, Ḳ *.) Andقَصَرَةٌ↓, (Ḳ,) orقَصَرٌ↓, without ة, accord. to the Nawádir of IAạr, as cited in the L, and so in the handwriting of Ṣgh, (TA,) andقَصَارٌ↓, (Ḳ,) signify Laziness; slothfulness.
