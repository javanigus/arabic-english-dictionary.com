كشث كشح كشر

1. ⇒ كشح

كَشَحَ لَحُ بِالعَدَاوَةِ; andكاشحهُ↓, (Ṣ, L, Ḳ,) inf. n. مُكَاشَحَةٌ and كِشَاحٌ; (L;) signify the same, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) He determined, or resolved, upon enmity to him: (L:) was inimical to him, (L, Ḳ,) and broke friendship with him: (L:) hated him and was inimical to him: bore a secret enmity to him. (L.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

كَشَحَ He dispersed, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and drove away, (Ṣ,) a people. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B2

كَشَحُوا عَنِ المَآءِ, andانكشحوا↓, They (a people, Ṣ) dispersed from the water: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) they went away from it, and dispersed: (TA:) they went back from it. (T.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B3

كَشَحَ He went back; retired. (A.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

كَشَحَهُ He pierced, or stabbed, him in the part called كَشْحٌ. (TA.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ― Signification: C2

كَشَحَ; (L;) andكشّح↓, (Kr, L,) inf. n. تَكْشِيحٌ; (Ḳ;) He cauterized, (Ḳ,) or marked with a hot iron, a camel (L) in the part called كَشْحٌ. (L, Ḳ.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ― Signification: C3

كُشِحَ, inf. n. كَشْحٌ, He had a pain in his كَشْحٌ. (L.)

Root: كشح - Entry: 1. ― Signification: C4

Also, (inf n. as above, Ṣ,) He was cauterized for the disease called كَشَحٌ. (Ṣ, L, Ḳ.)

كَشْحٌ

كَشْحٌ The حَشَى [i. e. the flank; or part between the false ribs and the hip; also explained in the TA by the word خَاصِرَة]: (L:) or the part between the hypochondre and the false ribs, (Ṣ, L, Ḳ,) and from near the navel to the portion of flesh and sinew next the back-bone: (L:) the part against which the sword hangs when a man is wearing it; and the part corresponding thereto on the other side: (T:) or each of the two sides of the belly, externally and internally, [or behind and before]; and so in a horse: (M:) or the waist: [see a verse of Imra-el-Ḳeys cited voce مُذَلَّلٌ:] or the part from the top, or projecting portion, of the haunch-bone to the armpit: (L:) pl. كُشُوحٌ. (A.)

Root: كشح - Entry: كَشْحٌ ＝ Dissociation: B

طَوَى كَشْحًا He determined upon a thing, or affair, and persevered in his determination. (T.) [See an ex. voce أَبَّ.]

Root: كشح - Entry: كَشْحٌ ― Signification: B2

طَوَى كَشْحَهُ عَلَى الأَمْرِ He determined, or resolved, upon the thing, or affair, in his mind, syn. أَضْمَرَهُ: (Ṣ, A, Ḳ:) and hid it, or concealed it: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) or, accord. to the L, and other lexicons, he persevered in the thing, or affair. (TA.)

Root: كشح - Entry: كَشْحٌ ― Signification: B3

طَوَى كَشْحَهُ He went away, and cut the ties of kindred. (L.)

Root: كشح - Entry: كَشْحٌ ― Signification: B4

طَوَى كَشْحَهُ عَنِّى He cut me, or cut the tie of friendship that united him to me, and broke off from me, abandoned me, or discarded me, (Ṣ, L, Ḳ,) and became inimical to me: (L:) he turned away from me. (L.)

Root: كشح - Entry: كَشْحٌ ― Signification: B5

كَشْحٌ Either side of a وِشَاح; from which the كشح of the body is said to have its name, because the former hangs against the latter: (L:) or a وِشَاح [altogether]; so called because it hangs against the كشح of the body; in like manner as an إِزَار is called حَقْوٌ: (A:) pl. كُشُوحٌ (TA.)

Root: كشح - Entry: كَشْحٌ ＝ Dissociation: C

The kind of shells called cowries, or conchæ Veneris; syn. وَدَعٌ: pl. كُشُوحٌ: (Ḳ:) or a وِشَاح made of such shells: أَوْشِحَة were made of white shells of that kind. (Aboo-Saʼeed Es-Sukkaree.)

كَشَحٌ

كَشَحٌ A certain disease (which attacks a man, Ṣ, Ḳ,) in the كَشْح, (meaning the flank, خَاصِرَة, TA,) and for which he is cauterized: (Ṣ, L, Ḳ:) or the pleurisy, syn. ذَاتُ الجَنْب. (Ḳ.)

كُشَاحَةٌ

كُشَاحَةٌ, a subst., A determining, or resolving, upon enmity to another: hating enmity: secret enmity: estrangement of oneself from another. (L.)

كِشَاحٌ

كِشَاحٌ A mark made by burning with a hot iron in the part called كشح. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

كَاشِحٌ

كَاشِحٌ One who determines, or resolves, upon enmity (يُضْمِرُهُ) to another: (Ṣ, L, Ḳ:) a hating enemy: (L:) a secret enemy: as though he folded up enmity in his كَشْح; or as though he turned his كَشْح towards thee, and his face from thee; (ISd, L;) or because he conceals enmity in his كَشْح, in which is his liver, which is the seat of enmity and hatred; wherefore an enemy is called أَسْوَدُ الكَبِدِ, as though enmity had burned his liver: (L:) or, accord. to some, one who estranges himself from another. (Mṣb.)

مَكْشُوحٌ

مَكْشُوحٌ A man cauterized for the disease called كَشَحٌ: (Ṣ, L, Ḳ:) a man cauterized with the mark called كِشَاحٌ, below the ribs. (L.)

Root: كشح - Entry: مَكْشُوحٌ ― Signification: A2

A man smitten with a sword in his كَشْح. (Ḳ.)
