كمفطس كمل كمن

1. ⇒ كمل

كَمَلَ He, or it, was, or became, perfect; and sometimes, it was, or became, complete, entire, whole, or full; which latter signification is more properly expressed by تَمَّ, which see.

كَاملٌ

كَاملٌ Complete with respect to bodily vigour, having attained the usual term thereof. See لَيْثُ عِفِرِّينَ, art. عفر. Also Consummate, perfect.

الكَمَالِ

عَيْنُ الكَمَالِ: see فَقَأَ.

مُكَمِّلَاتُ

مُكَمِّلَاتُ Supererogatory acts of religious service.
