كُبٌّ / كُبَّةٌ

كُبٌّ, [coll. gen. n., A plant or tree, of the kind called] حَمْض; (Ḳ;) a kind of tree excellent for kindling, the leaves of which make the tails of horses beautiful and long; it has joints and thorns, and grows in fine, or soft, and plain soil: n. un. with ة {كُبَّةٌ}: or, accord. to some, it is [a plant, or tree,] of the kind called نَجِيلُ الفَلَاةِ: but IAạr says, among the [plants, or trees, called] حَمْض, are the نجيل and the كبّ. (TA.)

كَبَّةٌ

كَبَّةٌ andكُبَّةٌ↓ A charge, an assault, or an onslaught, in war. (Ḳ) [And] كَبَّةٌ (Ṣ, Ḳ) andكُبَّةٌ↓ (Ḳ) A single impetus [in some copies of the Ṣ, دَفْعَة; in others, and in my copies of the Ḳ, دُفْعَة: I prefer the former reading:] in fighting and in running [in the CK, والجَرْىُ, which is doubtless a mistake]: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) and vehemence thereof. (TA.)

Root: كب - Entry: كَبَّةٌ ― Signification: A2

كَبَّةٌ andكُبَّةٌ↓ A collision between two troops of horses: in the Ḳ, بِيْنَ الجَبَلَيْنِ; but correctly, بَيْنَ الخَيْلَيْنِ, as in other lexicons. (TA.)

Root: كب - Entry: كَبَّةٌ ― Signification: A3

كَبَّةٌ (Ṣ, Ḳ) andكُبَّةٌ↓ (Ḳ) A letting loose, or setting free, horses, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) upon the race-course, or field, to run, or to charge. (Ṣ.) [This is evidently meant in the Ṣ as an explanation of the words rendered here “a single impetus”, &c.]

Root: كب - Entry: كَبَّةٌ ― Signification: A4

كَبَّةٌ (Ṣ, Ḳ) andكُبَّةٌ↓ (Ḳ) The vehemence and assault [in some copies of the Ṣ, دَفْعَة: in others, and in my copies of the Ḳ, دُفْعَة: I prefer the former reading:] of winter. (Ṣ, Ḳ)
