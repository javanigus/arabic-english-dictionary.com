كردم كرز كرس

كُرْزٌ

كُرْزٌ The [double bag, or double sack, called] خُرْج (ISk, Ṣ, Ḳ) of the pastor, (Ḳ,) in which he carries his provisions and utensils, and which is also put upon the back of the [ram called] كَرَّاز: (TA:) or a جُوَالِق [or sack]: (A, Mṣb:) or a small جوالق: (TA:) pl. [of pauc.] أَكْرَازٌ (ISd, TA) and [of mult.] كِرَزَةٌ. (Ṣ, Ḳ.) [See بَطِينٌ.]

كَرَّازٌ

كَرَّازٌ The ram that carries the خُرْج [i. e. the كُرْز q. v.] of the pastor: (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ:) he goes before the people, (TA,) and has no horns; (Ṣ, Mṣb;) because that which has horns (الأَقْرَنُ) diverts himself with smiting others with his horns. (Ṣ.)

Supplement:

3. ⇒ كارز

فلان يُكَارِزُ عن الحق الى الباطل, inf. n. مُكَارَزَةٌ: i. q. يُعَاجِزُ, expl. in art. عجز. (TA, art. عجز.)
