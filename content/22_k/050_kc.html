كدم كذ كذب

1. ⇒ كذّ

كَذَّ, [aor. ـِ {يَكْذِذُ},] (Ḳ,) inf. n. كَذٌّ, (TA,) It (a thing, TA) was, or became, rough, (Ḳ,) and hard. (TA.)

4. ⇒ اكذّ

اكذّوا They, (a people, Mṣb,) became among stones such as are termed كَذَّان. (L, Mṣb, Ḳ.)

كَذَّانٌ / كَذَّانَةٌ

كَذَّانٌ Soft stones, (AA, Ṣ, M, L, Mṣb, Ḳ,) as also جَذَّانٌ, (Aṣ, L in art. جذ,) like dry pieces of clay, (Ṣ, L, Mṣb, Ḳ,) and foraminous, or pierced with holes, (M, L,) or sometimes pierced with holes: n. un. with ة {كَذَّانَةٌ}. (L, Mṣb.) Some say, that the ن is a radical letter; (L, Mṣb;) but the form of the verb أَكَذَّ is against their assertion; for if the ن were so, it would appear in the verb. (Mṣb.)

كَذْكَذَةٌ

كَذْكَذَةٌ Intense redness. (Ḳ.)
