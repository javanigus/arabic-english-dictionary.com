1. ⇒ عرج

عَرَجَ, (Ṣ, A, O, Ḳ,) aor. ـُ {يَعْرُجُ}, (Ṣ,) inf. n. عُرُوجٌ (Ṣ, O, Ḳ) and مَعْرَجٌ, (O, Ḳ,) He ascended, or mounted. (Ṣ, A, O, Ḳ.) So in the saying عَرَجَ فِى الدَّرَجَةِ and فى السُّلَّمِ [He ascended, or mounted, the stair, or the series of steps, and the ladder]. (Ṣ, O.) And عَرَجَ فِى الشَّىْءٍ, and عَلَيْهِ, aor. ـُ {يَعْرُجُ} and ـِ {يَعْرِجُ}, inf. n. عُرُوجٌ, He ascended, or mounted, upon the thing (TA.) And عُرِجَ بِهِ means He was taken up to a high place; as, for instance, إِلَى عَنَانِ السَّمَآءِ [to the clouds of Heaven]. (Ḥam p. 87.)

Root: عرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

And عَرَجَ الشَّىْءُ The thing became high, or elevated. (TA.)

Root: عرج - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

عَرَجَ, (Ṣ, O, Mṣb, Ḳ,) with fet-ḥ to the ر, (O,) aor. ـُ {يَعْرُجُ}, inf. n. عَرْجٌ; (Mṣb; [accord. to the O عَرَجٌ;]) or عَرَجَ; and عَرِجَ and عَرُجَ; (Ḳ;) He limped, or had a slight lameness, (Ṣ, O, Mṣb, Ḳ,) and walked like the lame, (Ṣ, O,) by reason of some accident that had befallen him (Ṣ, O, Mṣb, Ḳ) in his leg or foot, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ.) not naturally, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) or not by reason of a chronic ailment: (Mṣb:) or عَرَجَ, aor. ـُ {يَعْرُجُ} and عَرِجَ and عَرُجَ; inf. n. عَرَجَانٌ; he walked like the lame, with a limping gait, by reason of some accident. (L.)

Root: عرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B2

And عَرِجَ, (Ṣ, O, Mṣb, Ḳ,) aor. ـَ {يَعْرَجُ}, (Mṣb, Ḳ,) inf. n. عَرَجٌ (Ṣ,* O,* Mṣb, Ḳ,* TA) and عُرْجَةٌ, (TA,) He was lame, walked lamely, or limped, (Ṣ, O, Mṣb, Ḳ,) naturally, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ,) or by reason of a chronic ailment: (Mṣb:) or he became lame. (TA.) [See also عَرَجٌ below.]

Root: عرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B3

عَرَجٌ also signifies The setting of the sun: or its inclining towards the place of setting: (Ṣ, O, Ḳ:) inf. n. of عَرِجَتْ. (TḲ.)

Root: عرج - Entry: 1. ― Signification: B4

And عَرِجَ, inf. n. عَرَجٌ, He (a camel) emitted his urine indirectly: said of the male only, when the hind girth is bound upon him [so as to press upon his sheath]: like حقِبَ. (TA.)

2. ⇒ عرّج

عرّج, inf. n. تَعْرِيجٌ, He made (a building, or structure, Ṣ, O, and a river, or rivulet, TA) to incline. (Ṣ, O, Ḳ, TA.)

Root: عرج - Entry: 2. ＝ Dissociation: B

عَرَّجْتُ عَنْهُ I turned from it, and left it, or forsook it; as alsoانعرجت↓ عنه. (Mṣb.)

Root: عرج - Entry: 2. ― Signification: B2

And عرّج عَلَيْهِ He bent, or inclined, to, or towards, him, or it. (TA.) You say, مَرَّ بِهِ فَمَا عَرَّجَ عَلَيْهِ [He passed by him, or it,] and did not bend, or incline, to him, or it. (A.) [But this may be otherwise rendered, as is shown by what follows.]

Root: عرج - Entry: 2. ― Signification: B3

عرّج also signifies He remained, stayed, abode, or dwelt; (Ḳ, TA;) as alsoتعرّج↓. (T, TA.) You say, عرّج بِالمَكَانِ He remained, stayed,, &c., in the place. (TA.) And عرّج عَلَى الشَّىْءِ, (O,) inf. n. as above, (Ṣ, A,) He remained, stayed, or abode, intent upon the thing; (Ṣ, A, O;) as alsoتعرّج↓ عليه. (O.) See also عُرْجَةٌ, in two places: and see 2 in art. عوج. And مَا عَرَّجْتُ عَلَى الشَّىْءِ means I did not pause, or stop, at the thing: (Mṣb: [and the like is said in the Mgh:]) or I did not care for it, or regard it. (TA in art. وبر.) And عرّج عَلَى المَنْزِلِ, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ,) andتعرّج↓, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) He confined his camel that he rode at the place of alighting or abode, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ,) and remained, or stayed: (Ṣ, O:) or تَعْرِيجٌ signifies the confining the camel that one rides, remaining, or staying, for one's travelling-companions or for some object of want: and عرُج النَّاقَةَ means he confined the she-camel. (TA.)

6. ⇒ تعارج

تعارج [He pretended to be lame;] he imitated the gait of a lame person. (TA.)

7. ⇒ انعرج

انعرج It (a thing, Ṣ, Mṣb) bent or inclined; (Ṣ, O, Mṣb, TA;) and so a road: (TA:) and it was, or became, curved, or crooked. (Mgh.) You say, انعرج بِنَا الطَّرِيقُ [The road bent, or inclined, with us]. (A.) And انعرج عَنِ الطَّرِيقِ He declined from the road: (Mgh:) and انعرج الرَّكْبُ عَنْ طَرِيقِهِمْ [The company of riders declined from their road]. (A.) See also 2, second sentence.

مِعْرَاجٌ

مِعْرَاجٌ (Ṣ, A, O, Ḳ) andمِعْرَجٌ↓ andمَعْرَجٌ↓, (Ṣ, O, Ḳ,) the second and third allowable accord. to Akh, like مِرْقَاةٌ and مَرْقَاةٌ, (Ṣ, O,) A ladder, or series of steps or stairs: (Ṣ, A, O, Ḳ:) or, with the article ال, [but most commonly the first of these with ال,] a thing resembling a دَرَجَة [i. e. ladder, or series of steps or stairs], upon which the souls ascend when they are taken [from their bodies]: it is said that there is nothing more beautiful than it; so that when the soul sees it, it cannot refrain from making its exit [from the body]: (TA:) hence لَيْلَةُ المِعْرَاجِ [the Night of the Ladder; in which Moḥammad is related to have ascended from Jerusalem to Heaven, after having been conveyed to the former from Mekkeh upon the beast named البُرَاق]: pl. مَعَارِيجُ and مَعَارِجُ, like مَفَاتِيحُ and مَفَاتِحُ.
