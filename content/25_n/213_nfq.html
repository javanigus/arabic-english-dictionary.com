نفع نفق نفل

1. ⇒ نفق

نَفَقَتِ السُّوقُ The marked became brisk, its goods selling much; syn. قَامَت. (Ḳ.)

Root: نفق - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

نَفَقَ It was, or became, saleable; easy, or ready, of sale; or in much demand: see its syn. رَاجَ.

Root: نفق - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

نَفَقَتْ It (a commodity, سِلْعَة,) was in much demand: and she (a woman) was demanded in marriage by many. (Mṣb.)

Root: نفق - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

نَفِقَتِ الدَّراَهِمُ, inf. n. نَفَقٌ, The dirhems passed away, came to an end, or became spent or exhausted; syn. نَفِدَت. (Mṣb.)

3. ⇒ نافق

نَافَقَ He played the hypocrite in religion: (Ḳ, TA:) he pretended, to the Muslims, that he held the religion of El-Islám, concealing in his heart another religion than El-Islám. (Mṣb.) And نَافَقَ فُلاَنًا He acted with such a one hypocritically. (TḲ in art. دهن. [But I have not found this elsewhere.]) And نَافَقَ فِى المَحَبَّةِ [He acted the hypocrite in respect of love]. (Ḥar, p. 505.) See خَانَ.

4. ⇒ انفق

أَنْفَقَ He expended money: and he (God or a man) dispensed gifts.

5. ⇒ تنفّق

تَنَفَّفَتِ الجَزُورُ [The slaughtered camel became dealt out, or dispensed]. (Ṣ, Ḳ in art. شيط.)

Root: نفق - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A2

تَنَفَّقَ: see Ḥar, p. 472.

Root: نفق - Entry: 5. ― Signification: A3

تَنَفَّقَ It (a wound) cracked in its sides, and made, in the flesh, what resembled أَنْفَاق↓, i. e. holes in the ground, or subterranean excavations or habitations, pl. of نَفَقٌ. (TA in art. دسم.)

نَفَقٌ

نَفَقٌ: see سَرَبٌ

Root: نفق - Entry: نَفَقٌ ― Signification: A2

أَنْفَاقٌ The hole of rats or mice. (Ṣ, TA in art. خفى:) see 1 in that art.: holes in the ground; or subterranean excavations or habitations; pl. of نَفَقٌ. (TA in art. دسم.) See 5.

Root: نفق - Entry: نَفَقٌ ＝ Dissociation: B

Also Fresh olive-oil: see فَاقٌ in art. فوق: also mentioned in art. نفق in the TA.

نَفَقَةٌ

نَفَقَةٌ What one expends, of money and the like, (Ḳ, TA,) upon himself and upon his family or household. (TA.)

نَيْفَقٌ

نَيْفَقٌ The part of a pair of drawers, or trousers, which is turned down at the top, and sewed, and through which the waistband, or string, passes. See نُقْبَة.
