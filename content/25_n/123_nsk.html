نسف نسك نسل

1. ⇒ نسك

نَسَكَ He worshipped: used transitively. See an ex. in a verse of El-Aạshà, in the Ṣ, art. نصب. See 5.

4. ⇒ انسك

أَنْسَكَ [app. He washed and purified a garment]. (TA voce إِجْتَابَ.)

5. ⇒ تنسّك

تَنَسَّكَ He devoted himself to religious exercises; applied himself to devotion; (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ:) as alsoنَسَكَ↓ (Ṣ, Ḳ) andنَسُكَ↓: (Ḳ:) or the last, he became a نَاسِك. (Ṣ.)

نَسِيكَةٌ

نَسِيكَةٌ: see عَقِيقَةٌ.

مَنَاسِكُ

مَنَاسِكُ الحَجِّ The religious rites and ceremonies of the pilgrimage: or the places where those rites and ceremonies are performed. (Mṣb.)
