نزأ نزب نزح

1. ⇒ نزب

نَزَبَ, aor. ـِ {يَنْزِبُ}, inf. n. نَزِيبٌ (Ṣ, Ḳ) and نَزْبٌ and نُزَابٌ (Ḳ) He (an antelope) uttered a cry, or sound, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) at rutting-time. (Ṣ.) Used with reference to the buck and the doe; (Ḳ;) or the buck only. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

6. ⇒ تنازب

تنازبوا i. q. تنابزوا. (Ḳ.) Accord. to IHsh and others, this verb has not been heard [from the Arabs of the classical ages], but only the subst. نَزَبٌ, which is therefore decided to be a transp. form of نَبَزٌ. (TA.)

نَزَبٌ

نَزَبٌ A surname; a nickname; a name of reproach; an opprobrious appellation: syn. لَقَبَ: (Ḳ:) i. q. نَبَزٌ. (TA.) See 6.

نَيْزَبٌ

نَيْزَبٌ A buck-antelope; and a bull. (Ḳ.) [By the latter is probably meant the kind of antelope called بَقَرُ الوَحْشِ.]
