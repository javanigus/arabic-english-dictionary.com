صر صرب صرج

1. ⇒ صرب

صَرَبَ He made the sour milk termed صَرْب: (Ḳ,* TA:) [or] صَرَبَ اللَّبَنَ, (M, TA,) aor. ـِ {يَصْرِبُ}, (M,) or ـُ, (TA,) inf. n. صَرْبٌ, he made the milk to become what is termed صَرْب: and he milked some of the milk upon other milk, and left it to become sour: (M, TA:) or صَرَبَ اللَّبَنَ فِى الوَطْبِ he collected the milk in the skin, portion after portion, and left it to become sour; as alsoاِصْطَرَبَهُ↓: (Ṣ:) or you say, صَرَبَ اللَّبَنَ فِى السِّقَآءِ and السَّمْنَ فِى النِّحْىِ [he collected, portion after portion, and left, the milk in the skin called سِقَآء and the clarified butter in the skin called نِحْى]. (M, TA.)

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

[Hence,] صَرَبْتُ اللَّبَنَ فِى الضَّرْعِ † I caused the milk to collect in the udder, not drawing it forth. (Ḳṭ, TA.)

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

And صَرَبَ † He kept in, or retained, and collected, [his] urine: (Ḳ, TA:) or withheld it long: and accord. to some, particularly said of a stallion-camel: (TA:) [or] صَرَبَ بَوْلَهُ, (Ṣ, M,) aor. ـُ {يَصْرُبُ} and ـِ {يَصْرِبُ}, inf. n. صَرْبٌ, (M,) † he kept in, or retained, and collected, his urine: (Ṣ, M:) accord. to some, particularly said of a stallion-camel. (M.)

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

And صَرَبَ الصَّبِىُّ [ذَا بَطْنِهِ being app. understood] † The boy remained some days without discharging his excrement, or ordure: (M, TA:) and صَرَبَ بَطْنُ الصَّبِىِّ, inf. n. صَرْبٌ, † the boy's belly became constipated (عَقَدَ), that he might become fat: (M:) [or] صَرَبَ الصَّبِىُّ لِيَسْمَنَ † the boy's excrement, or ordure, (ذُو بَطْنِهِ,) became confined, so that he remained a day without discharging it, when he was about to become fat. (Ṣ.) [In the copies of the Ḳ, صَرَبَ is expl. as signifying عَقَدَ بَطْنَ الصَّبِىِّ لِيَسْمَنَ, to which, written without the syll. signs, the TA assigns the last of the meanings above; therefore, I doubt not, the right reading is بَطْنُ, agreeably with the explanation of صَرَبَ بَطْنُ الصَّبِىِّ in the M, given above: otherwise, the meaning must be † He, or it, caused the boy's belly to become bound, or constipated, that he might become fat.]

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

صَرِبَ, aor. ـَ {يَصْرَبُ}, (Ḳ, TA,) inf. n. صَرَبٌ, (TḲ,) said of milk, (TA,) It became collected (Ḳ, TA) in the udder. (TA.)

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

صَرَبَتِ الأَرْضُ The land produced herbs, or trees, such as are termed صَرَب [a coll. gen. n. of which the n. un. is صَرَبَةٌ, q. v.]. (M, Ḳ.* [The meaning is indicated in both by the context.])

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: D

صَرَبَ also signifies He cut, or cut off; (Ḳ, TA;) i. q. صَرَمَ; like as one says ضَرْبَةَ لَازِبٍ and لَازِمٍ. (TA.)

Root: صرب - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: E

And i. q. كَسَبَ [He gained, acquired, or earned;, &c.]. (Ḳ.)

2. ⇒ صرّب

تَصْرِيبٌ The drinking of sour milk, (O, Ḳ, TA,) such as is called صَرْب. (TA.)

Root: صرب - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A2

4. ⇒ اصرب

اصرب He (a man, TA) gave. (O, Ḳ.) One says, اصرب إِلَيْهِ مَالًا He (a man) gave to him property. (TḲ.)

اصطرب صَرْبَةً He provided, or took, for himself some milk, either fresh or sour, in a skin, for a journey. (M, TA.)

11. ⇒ اصرابّ

اصْرَابَّ [said by MF to be written by ISd اِصْرَأَبَّ↓] It was, or became, smooth, (M, O, Ḳ, TA,) and clear; (TA;) said of a thing, (M, Ḳ, TA,) or of milk. (O.)

صَرْبٌ

صَرْبٌ andصَرَبٌ↓ (Ṣ, M, &c.) Sour milk (M, Mgh, Ḳ) that has been collected in a skin: (M, Ḳ:) or very sour milk: (Ṣ, Mṣb:) or milk that has been collected in a skin for some days so that it has become very sour: (Aṣ, M:) n. un. صَرْبَةٌ↓ andصَرَبَةٌ↓: (M, TA:) صِرَابٌ, occurring in some of the Expositions of the Jámiʼ eṣ-Ṣagheer, is a mistranscription; or it may be a pl. of صَرْبٌ accord. to the analogy of حَبْلٌ and حِبَالٌ, and رَمْلٌ and رِمَالٌ. (Mgh.) One says,جَآءَنَا بَصَرْبَةٍ↓ تَزْوِى الوَجْهَ [He brought us some sour milk, or very sour milk, that had been collected in a skin, making the face to contract in wrinkles]. (Ṣ.) [SM here adds,] Az says that الصَّرْمُ is like الصَّرْبُ, and is better known. (TA. [But this evidently relates to the signification of “the act of cutting,” or “cutting off;” not to الصَّرْبُ as applied to milk.])

Root: صرب - Entry: صَرْبٌ ― Signification: A2

Also, (Ḳ,) or the former word, (M,) Milk that is provided in a skin for a journey, (M, Ḳ,) whether fresh or sour. (M.)

Root: صرب - Entry: صَرْبٌ ― Signification: A3

And both words, (M, Ḳ, TA,) orصَرَبٌ↓ [only], (Ṣ, Mṣb,) Gum: (Mṣb:) or red gum: (T, Ṣ, M, L, TA: in the Ḳ, الصِّبْغُ الأَحْمَرُ is erroneously put for الصَّمْغُ الأَحْمَرُ: TA:) some say (M) it is the gum of the طَلْح (Ṣ, M) and of the عُرْفُط, peculiarly; the pieces of which are red, as though they were ingots [of gold], and they are broken with stones: (M:) the n. un. is صَرَبَةٌ↓: (Ṣ, M: [صَرْبَةٌ as a n. un. in this sense I do not find mentioned:]) and sometimes it [i. e. صَرْبٌ or صَرَبٌ] has صِرَابٌ for pl.: (M:) sometimes, (Ṣ,) what is called صَرَبَةٌ↓ is a thing like the head of the cat [in size], within which is a thing [or substance] like دِبْس [or honey of dates], (Ṣ, Ḳ,) and like glue, (Ṣ,) which is sucked and eaten. (Ṣ, Ḳ.)

صِرْبٌ

صِرْبٌ A few tents (بُيُوت [in the O, erroneously, بُتُوت]) of the weak sort of the Arabs of the desert: (IAạr, O,* Ḳ, TA:) and so صِرْمٌ. (O.)

صَرَبٌ

صَرَبٌ: see صَرْبٌ, in two places.

Root: صرب - Entry: صَرَبٌ ― Signification: A2

Also Red honey. (TA in art. ضرب.)

Root: صرب - Entry: صَرَبٌ ― Signification: A3

See also صَرَبَةٌ.

صَرْبَةٌ

صَرْبَةٌ: see صَرْبٌ, in two places.

Root: صرب - Entry: صَرْبَةٌ ― Signification: A2

Hence, ‡ The water [by which is meant the seminal fluid] that collects in the back [of a man]; as being likened to the [sour] milk that is collected in a skin. (M, TA.)

صَرَبَةٌ

صَرَبَةٌ: see صَرْبٌ, in three places.

Root: صرب - Entry: صَرَبَةٌ ― Signification: A2

Also A herb, (M, Ḳ,) and tree, (M,) that becomes green, and puts forth leaves, when dry, or that has grown, or become somewhat restored to a good state, after having been eaten [or depastured], (يَتَجَبَّرُ, so in the M, [in the Ḳ يَتَخَيَّرُ, which, as is remarked in the TḲ, is a mistake,]) after men [have fed their cattle therefrom]: pl. [or rather coll. gen. n.] صَرَبٌ↓. (M.)

صَرْبَى

صَرْبَى, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) accord. to Saʼeed Ibn-El-Museiyib, (TA,) The [she-camel called] بَحِيرَة, (Ṣ, Ḳ,) whose milk was forbidden [by the pagan Arabs] for the sake of the idols (الطَّوَاغِيت), no man milking her [for himself]: (TA:) thus called, (Ṣ, Ḳ, TA,) it is said, (TA,) because they used not to milk her save for the guest, so that her milk became collected [and retained] (Ṣ, Ḳ, TA) in her udder: (Ṣ, TA:) Ḳṭ says, it is from صَرَبْتُ اللَّبَنَ فِى الضَّرْعِ [expl. above]; or, as some say, from [الصَّرْبُ as signifying] “the act of cutting,” or “cutting off;” and this seems to be the more correct of the two explanations: accord. to IAạr, it signifies a she-camel having the ear slit, like the بَحِيرَة, or cut off: and its pl., he says, is صُرْبٌ. (TA.)

صِرَابٌ

صِرَابٌ See-produce, or corn, which has been sown after that which has been carried off to the place where it is trodden in the autumn. (O, Ḳ.)

صَرِيبٌ

صَرِيبٌ Milk that has been made what is termed صَرْب; as alsoمَصْرُوبٌ↓: (M, TA:) or sour milk: (A, Ḳ, TA:) pl. صُرْبٌ. (Ḳ.) So in the saying الضَّرِيبَ لَا الصَّرِيبَ, i. e. [Give me] the thick [milk], from a number of milch camels, that has been mixed together; not the sour. (A, TA.)

صَرَابَةٌ

صَرَابَةٌ Clearness, and smoothness: thus in the phrase صَرَابَةُ حَنْظَلٍ in a verse of Imra-el-Ḳeys; as some relate it: (M, TA:) as others relate it, صَرَايَة, (M,) or صَلَايَة. (TA.)

مِصْرَبٌ

مِصْرَبٌ A vessel in which milk is collected, portion after portion, and left to become sour: (Ṣ, Ḳ:*) and so مِكْرَصٌ and مِقْرَعٌ: pl. مَصَارِبُ. (TA.)

مَصْرُوبٌ

مَصْرُوبٌ: see صَرِيبٌ.

مُصَرَّبَةٌ

مُصَرَّبَةٌ † A she-camel that is kept from being milked, in order that she may become fat. (L in art. صفح, from the T.)
