صعب صعتر صعد

صَعْتَرٌ

صَعْتَرٌ I. q. سَعْتَرٌ [q. v.]: (Ḳ, and Ṣ and Mṣb in art. سعتر:) it grows in the country of the Arabs, and is of two kinds, سُهْلِىٌّ [i. e. of the plain] and جَبَلِىٌّ [i. e. of the mountain]: (AḤn, TA:) when strewn in a place, it drives away venomous or noxious reptiles and the like, (Ḳ,) such as serpents and scorpions. (TA.)
