صلخ صلد صلط

1. ⇒ صلد

صَلَادَةٌ and صُلُودَةٌ, [inf. ns. of which the verb is صَلُدَ,] used in relation to a stone [&c.], signify The being hard and smooth. (M.) [And صَلْدٌ has a similar meaning.] You say, صَلَدَتِ الأَرْضُ, andأَصْلَدَت↓, The land was, or became, hard: (Ḳ:) or ‡ so that it produced no plants, or herbage: (TA:) and صَلَدَ المَكَانُ, andأَصْلَدَ↓, (M, TA,) the place was, or became, hard: (TA:) or † produced no plants, or herbage. (M.) And صَلَدَ عَلَيْهِ الجَبَلُ, aor. ـِ {يَصْلِدُ}, inf. n. صَلْدٌ; and صَلُدَ, [aor. ـُ {يَصْلُدُ},] inf. n. صَلَادَةٌ and صُلُودَةٌ and صُلُودٌ; The mountain, or rock, baffled him, namely, a welldigger, [by its hardness,] and resisted his efforts. (M.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

[Hence,] صَلَدَ الزَّنْدُ, (M, Ḳ, and so in some copies of the Ṣ,) aor. ـِ {يَصْلِدُ}, inf. n. صَلْدٌ; (M;) or صَلِدَ, with kesr to the ل, aor. ـَ {يَصْلَدُ}, inf. n. صُلُودٌ; (AZ, Ṣ;) The زند [or piece of stick, or wood, for producing fire] gave a sound without emitting fire; (Ṣ, M, Ḳ;) andاصلد↓ signifies [the same, or] it emitted no fire. (Ḥam p. 407.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

And [hence,] صَلَدَتْ زِنَادُهُ [lit. “His pieces of stick, or wood, for producing fire, gave a sound without emitting fire”] means ‡ He was, or became, niggardly, tenacious, penurious, or avaricious: (AA, L, TA:) and صَلُدَ, alone, aor. ـُ {يَصْلُدُ}, (M, A, Ḳ,) inf. n. صَلَادَةٌ; (M, A;) and صَلَدَ, (M, A,) aor. ـِ {يَصْلِدُ}, (M,) or ـُ, (A,) inf. n. صَلْدٌ, (M,) or صُلُودٌ; (A;) andصلّد↓, inf. n. تَصْلِيدٌ; (Ḳ;) signify the same: (M, Ḳ:) or he was, or became, very niggardly, &c. (A.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A4

And صَلَدَتْ صَلَعَتُهُ, or صَلْعَتُهُ, (accord. to different copies of the Ḳ, in the TA the former,) The bald place on the front of his head shone, or glistened. (Ḳ, TA.) صَلَدَ is also used in the same sense, in a trad., in relation to milk flowing forth. (TA.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A5

And one says, جَآءَ بِمَرَقٍ يَصْلِدُ, and بِلَبَنٍ يَصْلِدُ, meaning He brought broth, and milk, containing little oily, or greasy, matter, and much water: for يَصْلِتُ. (T in art. صلت.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A6

صَلَدَتْ أَنْيَابُهُ His canine teeth caused a grating sound to be heard. (Ḳ,* TA.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A7

صَلَدَ بِيَدَيْهِ He clapped with his hands. (M.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A8

صَلَدَتِ الدَّابَّةُ, aor. ـِ {يَصْلِدُ}, (Ḳ,) inf. n. صَلْدٌ, (TA,) The beast beat the ground with its fore feet in its running. (Ḳ.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A9

صَلَدَ, (M,) or صَلَدَ فِى الجَبَلِ, (Ḳ,) aor. ـِ {يَصْلِدُ}, inf. n. صَلْدٌ, said of a mountain-goat, (M,) He ascended the mountain. (M, Ḳ.)

Root: صلد - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A10

صَلْدٌ

صَلْدٌ Hard and smooth; (Ṣ, M, A, Ḳ;) as alsoصِلْدٌ↓ (Ḳ) andصَلُودٌ↓ (M) andصَلِيدٌ↓ (A) andأَصْلَدُ↓; (M;) applied to a stone; (Ṣ, M, A;) and so the first applied to land or ground (أَرْض); (Ṣ;) and to a solid hoof, as alsoصِلْدِمٌ↓ andصُلَادِمٌ↓, which last is of the measure فُعَالِمٌ accord. to Kh, but فُعَالِلٌ accord. to others; (M;) and to a side of the forehead, (Ṣ, M,) or thus applied meaning smooth and tough; (L;) and to a head, as alsoصُلَادِمٌ↓, (M,) or thus applied meaning ‡ upon which no hair grows: (A:) andصَلَوْدَدٌ↓, (M, Ḳ,) which is of an extr. form, (M,) has the first of the significations above, (Ḳ,) or signifies [simply] hard: (M:) the pl. of صَلْدٌ (M, L) and of صَلُودٌ↓ (M) is أَصْلَادٌ. (M, L.)
