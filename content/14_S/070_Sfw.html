1. ⇒ صفو ⇒ صفى

صَفَا, (Ṣ, M, Mṣb,) aor. يَصْفُو, (Ṣ, Mṣb,) inf. n. صَفَآءٌ (Ṣ, M, Mṣb, Ḳ *) and صُفُوٌّ (M, Mṣb, Ḳ *) and صَفْوٌ (Ḳ,* TḲ) and صَفْوَةٌٰ and صِفْوَةٌ, (MA,) said of wine, or beverage, (Ṣ,) or of water, (TḲ,) or of a thing, (M,) It was, or became, clear, limpid, or pure; contr. of كَدِرَ; (Ṣ, M, Ḳ;*) or free from الكَدَر [i. e. turbidness, thickness, or muddiness]; (Mṣb;) or free from admixture. (Er-Rághib, TA.) And, said of the air, or atmosphere, It was, or became, cloudless; free from any particle of cloud. (M, Ḳ.) [And it is also said, tropically, of life; and of the mind, or heart; and of love, or affection;, &c.]
