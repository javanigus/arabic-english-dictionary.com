ضعو ضغث ضغط

1. ⇒ ضغث

ضَغَثَ الشَّىْءَ, aor ـَ, inf. n. ضَغْثٌ, He collected together the thing: whence ضِغْثٌ signifying “a handful of herbs, &c.” (Mṣb.) See also 2.

Root: ضغث - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A2

And [hence,] ضَغَثَ الحَدِيثَ, (A, Ḳ,) aor. as above, (Ḳ,) and so the inf. n., (Ṣ,) ‡ He confused, or confounded, [or related in a confused manner,] the tradition, or story, or the like. (Ṣ, A, Ḳ)

Root: ضغث - Entry: 1. ― Signification: A3

And ضَغَثَ الثَّوْبَ ‡ He washed the garment, or piece of cloth, without cleansing it, (O, Ḳ, TA,) so that it remained in a dubious state. (TA.)

Root: ضغث - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: B

ضَغَثَ السَّنَامَ, aor. as above, (Ḳ,) and so the inf. n., (Ṣ,) He felt the camel's hump in order to know whether it were fat or not: (Ṣ, Ḳ:) and ضَغَثَهَا he felt her [i. e. a she-camel] for that purpose. (TA.)

Root: ضغث - Entry: 1. ＝ Dissociation: C

ضَغَثَ, accord. to the Ḳ, [and the O, as on the authority of Fr,] is also said of a وَرَل, meaning It uttered a cry: but this is correctly with ب [i. e. ضَغَبَ]. (TA.)

2. ⇒ ضغّث

ضغّث النَّبَاتَ He made the plants, or herbage, what are termed أَضْعَاث [pl. of ضِعْثٌ]. (A, TA.)

Root: ضغث - Entry: 2. ― Signification: A2

[Hence,] ضغّث رَأْسَهُ † He poured water upon his head, and then divided the hair with his fingers into separate handfuls, in order that the water might reach to the skin. (L, TA.) [But see what follows.] It is said in a trad. of ʼÁïsheh, كَانَتْ تُضَغِّثُ رَأْسَهَا, (TA,) orتَضْغَثُ↓ رأسها, (so in the JM,) meaning She used to rub about the hair of her head with her hand, in washing, as though mixing it together, in order that the water with which she washed might enter into it. (TA.)

4. ⇒ اضغث

اضغث الرُّؤْيَا, said of a dreamer, ‡ He related the dream confusedly. (A, TA.)

8. ⇒ اضتغث ⇒ اضطغث ⇒ اضّغث

اضطغث ضِغْثًا He collected a handful of herbage, fresh and dry mixed together. (Ḳ.)

ضَغْثٌ

ضَغْثٌ The state of a thing's being confused, one part with another. (TA.)

ضِغْثٌ

ضِغْثٌ A handful of herbs, (AḤn, Ṣ, A, Mgh, Mṣb, Ḳ,) mixed together, (Ṣ, A, Mṣb, Ḳ,) fresh and dry: (Ṣ, Mṣb, Ḳ:) or a handful of twigs of trees or shrubs; (Mgh,* Mṣb;) or of fruit-stalks of the raceme of a palm-tree: (Mgh, Mṣb:) originally, a number of twigs all having one root or stem: and afterwards applied to what is collected together: (Mṣb:) or a thing that one collects together, such as a bundle of [the species of trefoil called] رَطْبَة; and of what has a stem, and grows tall: (Fr, TA:) or whatever is collected together, and grasped with the hand: (AHeyth, TA:) or a bundle of herbs mixed together; or of firewood: pl. أَضْغَاثٌ. (TA.) In the Ḳur xxxviii. 43, it is said to mean A bundle of rushes (أَسَل, so in the Mgh and the O, in my copy of the Mṣb اثل [which I think a mistranscription, on account of what follows]), a hundred in number, (O, Mṣb,) consisting of slender stalks without leaves, (Mgh, Mṣb,) whereof mats are made. (Mṣb.) See also a prov. cited and expl. voce إِلَالَةٌ. Hence, in a trad., ضِغْثَانِ مِنْ نَارٍ, meaning ‡ Two bundles of lighted firewood. (TA.) And, in another trad., مِنْهُمُ الآخِذُ الضِّغْثَ, meaning † Among them is he who obtains somewhat of worldly goods. (TA.)

Root: ضغث - Entry: ضِغْثٌ ― Signification: A2

Also † What is confused, and without truth, or reality, [of dreams, and] of news, or tidings, and of an affair. (Sh, TA.) أَضْغَاثُ أَحْلَامٍ, [occurring in the Ḳur xii. 44 and xxi. 5,] of which the sing. is ضِغْثُ حُلْمٍ, (Mṣb,) means ‡ Complications of dreams; (A;) or medleys of dreams, falsely resembling true dreams: (O, Mṣb:) or a dream of which the interpretation will not prove true, because of its confusedness: (ISh, Ṣ, Ḳ:) or a false dream; the pl. form being used to give emphasis to the meaning of unreality, or because the phrase comprehends various things: (Bḍ in xii. 44:) or أَضْغَاثُ الرُّؤْيَا means the terrors, or frightful things, of the dream. (Mujáhid, O, TA.) One says also, أَتَانَا بِأَضْغَاثٍ مِنَ الأَخْبَارِ, meaning ‡ He brought us [various] sorts of news, or tidings. (TA.) ضِغْثٌ means also † A deed that is of a mixed kind, not pure, or not sincere. (IAth and O, from a trad.) And كَلَامٌ ضِغْثٌ † Speech in which is no good: pl. أَضْغَاثٌ. (TA.)

ضَغُوثٌ

ضَغُوثٌ, applied to a she-camel, i. q. ضَبُوثٌ; (Ṣ, Ḳ;) i. e. Of which one doubts whether she be fat, and which one therefore feels with his hand; (Ṣ;) or of which one feels the hump, in order to know whether she be fat or not: pl. ضُغْثٌ. (TA.) And A camel's hump of which one doubts whether it be fat or not. (Kr, TA.)

ضَغِيثَةٌ

ضَغِيثَةٌ A confused company of men. (O.)

ضَاغِثٌ

ضَاغِثٌ One who hides himself in a thicket or the like, and frightens boys by a sound reiterated in his fauces: (Ṣ:) the author of the Ḳ, following Ṣgh in the TṢ and O, and Az in the T, says that this is a mistake, and that the word is correctly written with ب [i. e. ضَاغِبٌ]; but IF and IM and others write it as in the Ṣ. (TA.)

تَضْغِيثٌ

تَضْغِيثٌ Rain that moistens the earth and the herbage. (Ḳ.)
